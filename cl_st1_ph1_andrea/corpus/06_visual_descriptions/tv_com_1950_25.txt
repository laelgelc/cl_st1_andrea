In black-and-white animation, three elaborately dressed women stand in a doorway inside a plain domestic room. They have exaggerated cartoon faces and smug smiles. One is tall and thin in a dark, formal-looking dress with puffed sleeves and a decorative headpiece; another is short and round in a patterned, ruffled skirt; the third is taller and broader, wearing a dark bodice and a light striped skirt. They cluster together, laughing and pointing toward the right, as if mocking someone out of sight.

The scene shifts to the person they are pointing at: a young woman presented as Cinderella. She has long dark hair tied back with a headband and bow, a worn dark dress with ragged sleeves, and a white apron tied behind her. She bends over a large rectangular sink, scrubbing the inside with a cloth. The sink is drawn as grimy and shaded, with wavy lines rising from it to suggest dirt or fumes. A small arched window hangs on the wall behind her. Cinderella looks tired and worried, with drooping eyelids and a downturned mouth, leaning heavily over the basin as she rubs the same dirty surface again and again.

A closer view emphasizes her anxious face: wide eyes, arched worried brows, and a small open mouth. She keeps scrubbing the sink, but the stains remain. As she continues, her exhausted face briefly changes into a doglike cartoon face with long floppy ears and a drooping muzzle, a visual joke showing how hard she is working. She still leans over the basin, one hand braced on the sink and the other pushing the cloth across the dirty surface.

Inside a cutaway view of the sink, a small impish character appears in the dark grime. He has pointy ears, a little cap, dark clothing, and a mischievous grin. He holds a rectangular sign with large block letters reading “I’M SINK SMOG.” He laughs and lounges in the dirty basin as if he owns it. Lines and circular bubbles sweep across the sink as Cinderella’s scrubbing passes over him, but he remains in the murky bottom, knocked around but not removed. The sign tips and falls partly into the grime.

Cinderella stops scrubbing and raises both hands in frustration beside the sink. She then covers her face with both hands, crying or despairing. The dirty sink is still in front of her, with the cloth draped over the edge. Over this sad image, a fantasy-like image fades in: a horse-drawn carriage rolls across a simple open background. A black horse pulls a small carriage with large wheels. A driver sits high in front, and the three formally dressed women ride in the carriage, smiling and waving as though on their way to a grand event. Cinderella is left behind at the sink.

A sparkling can of Bab-O appears by itself against a plain background. The cylindrical can has large white lettering reading “BAB-O” across the top front, with a small illustrated figure on the label and a small oval callout that is too small to read clearly. Starbursts flash around the can, making it look bright and magical. The can is animated with thin little legs, as if it is walking or dancing.

The sparkling Bab-O can appears near Cinderella at the sink. She now looks cleaner and more hopeful, holding both hands up in surprise as the can dissolves into a cloud of glittering stars. From the sparkles, a tiny fairy queen appears on the left. She has light hair, a small crown, wings, a full gown, and a wand tipped with a bright dot. Cinderella stands to the right of the sink, smiling with wide eyes and raised hands, watching the fairy. The fairy lifts her wand and sprinkles a shower of sparkles above the sink.

Cinderella then holds a can of Bab-O out in one hand, smiling. A close view shows the can resting on an open palm. The “BAB-O” name is large and clear on the label, and sparkles glint around the top. The top of the can has shaker holes. A hand tilts the can sideways, and a stream of white powder pours out through the perforated top.

The action returns to the inside of the sink, where the sink-smog imp stands in the dark basin. Bright white particles rain down over him. He looks startled as the cleaner falls around him. Soon white suds begin to form. A large hand or sweeping cleaning motion passes across the top of the basin, spreading foamy suds along the rim and down the sides. The foam grows thicker and whiter, surrounding the imp and pushing the dark grime back. The little smog character tumbles and flails inside the suds, partly buried by bubbles. The white foam swells until it fills the sink cutaway, and the dark smog shape is swallowed up. In closer views of the sink interior, mounds of bubbles fill the basin; the imp pops up in alarm, mouth open and arms raised, then is covered again and disappears among the foam.

Back outside the sink, Cinderella leans over the now bright, clean basin with her hands clasped under her chin, smiling in delight. A Bab-O can stands on the counter to the left of the sink. The faucet and handles are visible at the front edge, and the sink looks clean and light instead of dark and smoky. Cinderella raises both arms joyfully, beaming.

Her drab clothing transforms into a formal ball gown. The image changes to a ballroom-like scene with a checkered floor and small dancing couples in the background. Cinderella, now dressed in a strapless, full-skirted gown with a small crown or bow in her hair, holds out the side of her skirt gracefully. A prince-like man stands to her left, bowing toward her. He wears a dark formal outfit with a cape or fur-trimmed mantle and has light hair styled in a curled, royal-looking way. Cinderella smiles and shows him the clean, white result at her feet. Then she holds a can of Bab-O high in one hand while the prince bends and kisses her other hand.

In front of it, enormous white letters spelling “BAB-O” become characters with thin cartoon legs.

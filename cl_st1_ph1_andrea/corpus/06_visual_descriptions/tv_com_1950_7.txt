In black and white, the commercial opens outdoors in a sunny citrus grove. Orange trees fill both sides of a path, their dark leafy branches dotted with round fruit. Behind them is a low building with a curved tile roof, and a bright sun hangs above it. Large bold white lettering reading “Florida” appears across the grove, covering much of the center of the picture.

The grove view gives way to a closer arrangement of orange branches and fruit. In the middle, a tall drinking glass filled nearly to the rim with pale juice appears, framed by leaves and oranges. The word “Florida” is faintly printed on the glass. Bold white lettering grows over the glass and branches: “POWERHOUSE OF VITAMIN C,” with “POWERHOUSE” especially large and prominent.

The scene changes to an outdoor ice rink in a park-like city setting. Bare winter trees, benches, railings, and distant buildings line the background. On the ice, a figure skater in a dark skating dress and light-colored skates moves toward the viewer. Large white text at the bottom identifies her as “BARBARA ANN SCOTT.” She glides and jumps, arms raised, then the view emphasizes her lower legs and skates as the blades cross and carve over the smooth ice. A pale cloud of ice spray or mist briefly fills much of the picture as she passes close by, then her skates continue gliding across the rink.

The commercial moves into a tidy 1950s-style kitchen. A blonde adult woman with curled, shoulder-length hair stands at a built-in wall oven set into a brick-patterned wall. She wears a fitted light sweater with a high collar, a short pearl necklace, and dark lower clothing. Open shelving with dishes is visible to the right, cabinets to the left, and a decorative plate hangs on the wall. In the foreground, on a counter, sits a shiny rectangular tray holding several full glasses of orange juice and a can labeled “Orange Juice.” The woman turns away from the oven and comes forward to the counter, smiling toward the viewer as if presenting the tray.

She pauses behind the tray and speaks while facing front. The can stands upright in the center with tall glasses clustered around it, each filled with a foamy-topped light drink. She reaches down, takes one glass from the tray, and lifts it to chest height. Holding it in one hand, she looks at the glass, then toward the viewer, smiling. She raises it slightly like a toast, the full glass prominent beside her face.

Rows of canned orange juice then fill the picture. Several identical cans stand side by side on a store shelf, each with large dark words “Orange Juice,” a smaller “FLORIDA” label, and an illustration of an orange and a drinking glass near the bottom. A wire shopping basket or cart edge appears in the foreground. Hands reach in from above and from the side, grasp one can from the front row, and lift it out. The chosen can is held closer to the viewer, its “Orange Juice” and “FLORIDA” label clearly visible, while more cans remain lined up behind it.

The final display returns to a kitchen counter. A polished serving tray holds a central can of Florida orange juice surrounded by several filled glasses, their tops frothy and bright against the darker background. Large white lettering overlays the tray: “POWERHOUSE OF NATURAL VITAMIN C,” stretching across the glasses and can. As the words remain, the product arrangement stays centered: the can at the back or middle, glasses on both sides and in front, all neatly arranged for serving. The overlay fades to a closer view of the can and glasses, and the word “Florida” appears again in large white letters across the front, with a small sparkle-like highlight near the top of the lettering and the rim of a glass.

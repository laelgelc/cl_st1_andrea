The commercial opens in black and white with the single word “Triactin” printed in large, bold, dark letters on a plain grey background. The word gradually becomes larger and softer, filling more of the screen until it fades into a demonstration-style scene.

A man with a professional, lecturer-like appearance stands against a dark background. He appears middle-aged, wears a dark suit, white shirt, tie, and dark-rimmed glasses, and faces a large transparent medical illustration in front of him. The illustration shows a human nervous system drawn in bright white lines: a brain at the top, a spinal cord down the centre, and many branching nerves spreading through the shoulders, arms, chest, abdomen, and legs. The man gestures toward the drawing with one hand, as if explaining it. He moves closer behind or beside the transparent surface so that his face and suit are partly visible through the white nerve lines.

He focuses on the middle of the body, around the stomach area. A bold white circle is drawn over the abdomen, enclosing a dense cluster of nerve-like lines. His hand points inside the circle, then begins drawing additional thick white marks: angular, zigzag strokes and horizontal lines across the lower part of the circle. These marks sit on top of the anatomical drawing and visually emphasize disturbance or irritation in the stomach region. The man remains beside the diagram, looking from the marked abdomen area back toward the viewer, with a serious explanatory expression.

The image of the anatomy and the man gives way to a large product label filling the view. The label shows a prominent rounded “3” symbol with the words “Triple Action” inside it, and the brand name “TRIACTIN” printed below in large block letters. Along the lower part of the label, numbered claims are visible, including “1 Neutralizes Acid Stomach.” As more of the product display is revealed, a white bottle stands on the left and a rectangular box stands on the right. Both packages carry the same bold “3” Triple Action logo and Triactin branding. The bottle is upright with a short cylindrical cap; the box is labeled “TRIACTIN chewable tablets.” On both packages, three numbered lines list the product’s actions: “Neutralizes Acid Stomach,” “Relieves Digestive Tension,” and “Tranquilizes Nervous Stomach.” The packages rest on a simple tabletop against a plain grey studio background.

White on-screen text appears above and between the two products reading “Calms Stomach Spasms.” The bottle and chewable-tablet box remain side by side, cleanly lit, with the slogan hovering above them.

The anatomical illustration returns, superimposed behind the product display and then filling the view again. The stomach circle and the earlier jagged marks are visible over the network of nerves. A hand holding a white cloth enters and begins wiping across the marked stomach area. The cloth moves back and forth over the circle, rubbing away the thick white disturbance marks. As the hand wipes, the bold zigzags and bands fade or disappear, leaving the underlying nerve illustration clearer. The wiping motion continues across the left, centre, and right of the circled abdomen, visually suggesting the calming or clearing of the stomach disturbance.

A Triactin bottle then appears centred in front of the fading anatomy lines. The nervous-system drawing becomes faint in the background until the bottle stands alone on the tabletop. A metal spoon is lowered in front of the bottle by a hand from above. The spoon’s bowl hangs near the lower left of the bottle, shiny and reflective, indicating the liquid form of the medicine. The spoon is then left leaning upright against the bottle, its handle angled upward along the bottle’s front.

Attention shifts to the chewable-tablet box. The rectangular Triactin box sits on the tabletop, labeled “TRIACTIN chewable tablets” with the Triple Action logo and the same three numbered action lines. A hand reaches in from the right, opens the end flap, and pulls out a shiny blister pack. The pack contains four round white tablets arranged in a two-by-two grid, each sealed in a clear or reflective wrapper. As the pack is drawn out beside the box, the printed Triactin chewable-tablets branding is visible through or on the transparent packaging.

The commercial ends with the white liquid bottle on the left and the chewable-tablet box on the right, both facing forward on the plain tabletop. The words “Calms Stomach Spasms” appear again in white lettering above the products, while the Triactin name and Triple Action symbol remain clearly visible on the packaging.

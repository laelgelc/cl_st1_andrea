The commercial begins in black and white with the word “Triactin” centered on a plain gray background in large dark letters. The lettering fades and becomes indistinct, giving way to a medical-style illustration. A transparent front-facing human torso appears against a dark background, drawn mostly with thin white lines. The head, shoulders, arms, chest, and abdomen are outlined by a dense network of branching nerves. A bright white circle is drawn around the upper abdomen to call attention to the stomach area.

Inside the circled area, a small stomach-shaped organ is visible among the nerve lines. The stomach region is emphasized, then animated marks appear over it: sharp white zigzag lines and stacked horizontal bars fill the circle, visually suggesting irritation, spasms, or acid disturbance. At the right edge, a middle-aged, male-presenting demonstrator in a dark suit, white shirt, and tie steps partly into view beside the anatomical drawing. He faces the diagram and gestures toward the circled stomach area as if explaining it, his body angled toward the illustration rather than toward the viewer.

The diagram then blends into a close display of the product package. A large Triactin label fills the screen, with a bold, rounded “3” emblem at the top containing the words “Triple Action.” Beneath it, the name “TRIACTIN” appears in large block letters. Along the lower part of the label are small numbered rows describing the product’s actions, though much of the fine print is blurred; the first row visibly begins with “1” and “Neutralizes,” and the design clearly presents three listed effects. The product display widens to show two forms side by side on a simple tabletop: a white plastic bottle on the left, labeled Triactin, and a rectangular box on the right labeled “TRIACTIN chewable tablets.” Both packages use the same large “3” symbol and a three-item checklist layout.

The product images briefly appear over the anatomical nerve drawing, connecting the package to the stomach diagram. The view returns fully to the circled torso illustration, where the earlier zigzags and horizontal bars remain inside the stomach circle. A pale, soft-edged white mass then moves through the circled area, sweeping across the marks. It passes over the horizontal bars and zigzags repeatedly, as if wiping or neutralizing them. As it moves, the harsh marks become less prominent, and the circle’s contents look calmer and clearer, while the surrounding white nerve lines remain visible across the torso.

The demonstration then changes back to the product bottle alone on the tabletop. A metal spoon stands beside it, its bowl at the lower left and its handle leaning upward against the bottle, suggesting the liquid form is to be measured or taken by spoon. A hand enters from above and adjusts or places the spoon next to the bottle. The bottle’s label again shows the large “3” Triple Action logo and the word Triactin.

The focus shifts from the liquid bottle to the chewable-tablet box. The rectangular carton sits on the tabletop, with “TRIACTIN chewable tablets” printed across the front and a three-line action chart on the right side. A hand reaches in from the right, opens or pulls from the end of the carton, and slides out a shiny blister strip. Four round white tablets are visible in the strip, arranged in a two-by-two pattern, each sealed in a reflective pocket. The hand holds the strip partly out of the box, presenting the tablets clearly.

Near the end, the liquid bottle and the chewable-tablet box are shown together again on the plain tabletop, side by side. The bottle stands on the left and the box on the right. Large on-screen words appear above and between them reading “Calms Stomach Spasms,” reinforcing the final visual message while the two available Triactin forms remain displayed.

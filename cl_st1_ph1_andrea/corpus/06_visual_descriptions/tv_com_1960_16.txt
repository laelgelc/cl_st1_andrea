In black and white, a teenage girl stands at a row of school lockers in a hallway. She has a neat dark bob with heavy bangs and flipped ends, and wears a short-sleeved striped top tucked into a light skirt with a belt. As she reaches into an open locker, a teenage boy in a cardigan and open-collared shirt hurries up beside her. He leans in, looks at her hair, and lifts a lock near her shoulder between his fingers. She turns toward him with a displeased, questioning expression while he holds up his fingertips as if something has stuck to them. Another student’s face is visible in the open locker behind them, watching. The girl’s expression shifts to surprise, and she whips her head around, her glossy hair swinging broadly from side to side.

The hallway opens into a long school corridor lined with lockers, with an overhead sign reading “BASKETBALL” and smaller, less legible lettering beneath it. From the left, a theatrical witch-like woman appears. She is dressed in a tall pointed hat and a dark, full cape that spreads dramatically around her. The striped-shirt girl stands to the right, facing her. The witch gestures toward the girl, then moves closer as if taking charge of the problem.

A tight view of the girl’s hair shows the witch’s dark-gloved hand holding a fluffy white piece of cotton against the side of the girl’s head near her bangs. The cotton presses into the hair and clings there. When it is pulled away, wispy fibers remain stuck to the dark hair, making the stickiness visible. Back in the corridor, the witch faces the girl and makes a small magical-looking gesture. A pale, smoky puff or blur passes near the girl’s head. The girl glances upward and touches near her hair, reacting with surprise and curiosity.

The focus shifts to the girl’s hand held open beside her striped shoulder. A spray can suddenly appears in her grasp. Its patterned label reads “Hidden Magic,” with starburst-like graphics on the can and a light-colored cap. The girl smiles broadly and holds the can up near the side of her head. She sprays her hair, directing a visible mist from the can toward the smooth bob and the flipped ends. She keeps smiling as she applies the hairspray evenly along the side of her hairstyle.

The cotton test is repeated visually: the witch’s gloved hand again brings a fluffy white cotton pad close to the girl’s hair. The image overlaps briefly with the girl holding the can, connecting the product use with the test. Afterward, the scene shifts to the girl seated among other teenagers, likely in the same school setting near the basketball event. The boy sits close beside her, looking at her and reaching toward the end of her hair. The girl at first looks straight ahead, composed and serious, while people around them stand or sit in a crowded background. Then she turns toward the boy. As they look at one another, her expression softens into a smile, suggesting the problem has been solved and her hair is no longer troublesome.

The commercial ends with a large product display. The Hidden Magic can fills the screen, its swirling patterned label and large “Hidden Magic” name clearly visible. The can then appears standing upright with its pale cap on, placed on a simple display surface. To the right of the can, large block lettering reads: “THE PROBLEM SOLVING HAIR SPRAY.”

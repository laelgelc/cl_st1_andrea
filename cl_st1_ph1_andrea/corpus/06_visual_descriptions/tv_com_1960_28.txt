The commercial begins in a dark studio space washed in red light. A large BIZ carton fills the view in extreme close detail, often tilted so only parts of the package are visible. The box is grey-blue with huge red letters, and the words “WITH BIO-ENZYM” appear briefly along one edge, sometimes partly upside down because of the angled view. Near the bottom of the carton, a dark, fuzzy-edged, crescent-like shape moves along the package and over a pale mound or white area, suggesting a little “dirt eater” creature nibbling or biting. The visuals are stylised rather than realistic: the box, red lettering, black shadowy shape, and glowing highlights dominate against a black background.

The product is then shown more clearly as a full upright carton. The front reads “BIZ” in large red block letters, with “WITH BIO-ENZYM” above and “FOR PRE-SOAKING LAUNDRY” below. A white panel on the lower front contains a dark atom-like flower symbol. The packaging stands alone against a dark background, lit warmly so the red letters glow.

The commercial returns to a close side view of the box. On the right side of the dark screen, large white serif lettering appears in quotation marks: “Eats dirt.” The BIZ carton remains on the left, with the same small dark, arched shape at its lower edge, continuing the idea of the product as something that consumes stains.

A square white napkin or cloth is introduced in front of the package. It is covered with several irregular stains arranged around the surface: pink, reddish, and purplish blotches, with one especially dark red-brown stain near the center. The cloth fills more of the view, making the stains the focus. The central stain becomes very large and dark as it is shown up close, with uneven, feathered edges spreading into the fabric.

The stained cloth then appears hanging or held flat in a soaking setup, with a horizontal bar or rim visible near the top. The fabric looks wet and translucent under reddish lighting. The same stains are visible but appear diffused through the damp cloth, with the dark central blotch still prominent.

A pair of adult hands and forearms work inside a top-loading washing machine or laundry tub. The basin is filled with foamy water and pale fabric, and a central agitator rises from the middle. The hands press and move the cloth through the suds, then withdraw as the water and fabric continue to fill the tub.

Afterward, a clean white cloth is displayed upright against the dark background. The surface is smooth and evenly pale, with no visible stains remaining. A bare hand enters from below and gestures across the cloth, pointing and brushing lightly over the area as if showing that the stains are gone.

The scene changes to a neatly stacked pile of clean folded laundry. The stack includes towels and fabrics in white, cream, red, pink, and patterned designs, including stripes, checks, and textured bands. Behind the pile, the BIZ carton rises into view, standing upright like a sign above the clean laundry. The front of the package is visible again: large red “BIZ,” small “WITH BIO-ENZYM,” and “FOR PRE-SOAKING LAUNDRY,” with the dark atom-like symbol in the white panel.

The commercial ends by returning to the dark red product display. The BIZ carton is shown at the left, partly in close-up, and the white quoted words “Eats dirt” appear again on the right, reinforcing the final image of the product as a stain-eating pre-soak.

The commercial opens in black and white with a simple, centered drawing of a television picture tube seen from the side. It has a long narrow neck on the left and a large rounded bulb swelling out toward a circular face on the right, like an old cathode-ray tube. The tube slowly turns so its round screen faces forward. Inside the circular face appears a cartoon character: a short, very round, middle-aged-looking man with a bald head, small facial features, a dark suit jacket, a bow tie, and pale hands. His body nearly fills the circle, and his expression looks worried or uncomfortable.

The round cartoon man remains trapped inside the circular tube face. As the view widens, the tube is shown fitted into a large, old-fashioned wooden television cabinet. The cabinet is bulky and boxy, with a patterned speaker grille along the bottom, control knobs on the right, and curved decorative legs. The round picture tube sits awkwardly inside a rectangular cabinet opening, making the character look squeezed into a shape that does not fit him well. The set appears in a sparsely drawn room with framed wall decorations, and the cabinet looks large and clumsy in the open space.

The cabinet turns to show how deep and awkward it is. From the side, the television box is very thick, and the round tube’s body seems to take up a lot of space behind the screen. The cartoon man inside is shown bending and twisting as if cramped. In close views of the screen, he is pressed sideways and diagonally inside the rounded picture area, his arms and legs tucked in, his face frowning. The television cabinet itself tilts in the drawing, emphasizing the uncomfortable, misfit shape of the round tube inside the rectangular set.

The scene clears to a plain background. A small rounded rectangular picture area appears with the same cartoon man inside, still rotund but now contained in a more rectangular shape. The word “MOTOROLA” then appears large across the center of the screen in bold capital letters. A bright square grows behind the word, until the Motorola name sits clearly against a light rectangular field.

The cartoon man reappears in a rounded rectangular screen over the Motorola lettering. He starts as the same short, heavy figure, leaning forward with his hands down. Then his body changes: he stretches upward and becomes tall and slim, with long narrow legs and a smaller waist. His suit opens outward like a cape, revealing a light-colored vest or shirtfront with a bow tie. He poses confidently, arms spread wide, one leg lifted, as though showing off his new shape. The picture area around him is now a broad rounded rectangle rather than a circle.

The rectangular tube is then shown from the side. Its face is flatter and rectangular at the front, with the long narrow neck extending behind it. Compared with the earlier round bulb, it looks slimmer and more compact. The tube floats against a plain background and then appears inside a realistic living room setting. In this room, there is a brick wall, a framed picture above, curtains to the right, and a cushioned sofa or armchair on the right side. A wooden television cabinet stands near the brick wall on slender legs. The tube slides into the cabinet from the side, and the cabinet’s side panel closes around it, making the set look like a neat piece of furniture rather than an oversized box.

The finished Motorola television is shown in the living room. It is a compact wooden console on legs, with a large rounded-rectangle screen on the left and a vertical control panel with round dials on the right. The screen is bordered by a decorative frame. The animated slim man appears inside the screen, moving lightly across the picture area. He then stands in the center, wearing his bow tie and dark suit, smiling and gesturing with both hands near his face as if pleased with his new appearance.

Large text begins to appear across the television screen. The words “COLOR/65” become visible in tall, thin capital letters. The slim cartoon man remains faintly behind the lettering for a moment, then the lettering takes over the screen. A large stylized Motorola “M” emblem begins to form over the words. The animated man appears beside or partly behind the emblem, interacting with it like a stage prop, peeking and moving around its tall angular sides.

The final display fills the television screen with a large Motorola “M” logo centered above the words “MOTOROLA COLOR/65.” Beneath that, smaller text reads, “At your local dealer now.” The slim cartoon man is still visible near the right side of the logo, tiny compared with the big emblem, reinforcing the image of the new slimmer rectangular color television as the finished product.

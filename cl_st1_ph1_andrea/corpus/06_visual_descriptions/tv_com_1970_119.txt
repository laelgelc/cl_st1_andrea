A clean-shaven white man in his thirties or forties, identified by context as Alan Alda, stands in a bright outdoor public setting with people, umbrellas, and street-or-beachside shapes blurred behind him. He wears a pale blue long-sleeved collared shirt with a dark camera strap hanging against his chest. At first he looks down at a small black instant camera in his hands, examining it. He then looks up toward the viewer, smiling, and raises the camera slightly as if introducing it.

He turns the camera outward so its front faces us. The camera is compact and boxy, glossy black, with a round lens on the front and a small strap hanging from one side. He cradles it in one hand and gestures with it while speaking, then lowers his gaze back to the camera with a pleased smile. He holds it close to his chest, then brings it up again so the front is clearly visible, as though showing its size and simple shape.

Still smiling, he lifts the camera to eye level and aims it directly outward. One hand supports the bottom while the other steadies the side near his face. His right eye is partly hidden behind the camera as he peers through it, his expression playful and focused. He keeps the camera aimed forward for several moments, then a white-edged instant print begins to emerge from the bottom front of the camera. The new picture sticks out as a stiff rectangular card.

He lowers the camera and pulls the instant photograph free with his fingers. The print has a wide white border and a dark, nearly black image area that has not yet developed. Holding the camera in one hand and the fresh print in the other, he presents both side by side in front of his chest. The camera remains black and square on his right side, while the undeveloped print is held upright on his left, its white border bright against the softly colored background.

The dark print transitions into a fully visible color photograph. The instant photo fills the view: inside its white border are two children or young people wearing bright orange life jackets, apparently on or near a boat. One younger child on the left wears a light-colored brimmed hat and looks slightly squinting or serious; an older child with dark curly hair on the right smiles broadly, leaning in close. The orange life jackets dominate the picture, giving it a warm, vivid look.

Another finished instant photo appears, also held within a white Polaroid-style border. It shows three Black family members grouped closely together and smiling: an adult woman on the left in a green top, an adult man on the right wearing glasses and a reddish shirt, and a child in front between them. Their heads are close together, creating an intimate family snapshot.

The scene returns to the man in the blue shirt holding the Pronto camera out toward the viewer. He smiles directly ahead, then turns his head to his left as if responding to someone offscreen. A group of people appears facing him, crowded together in the open air, each holding a camera up to their face. They are a mixed group of adults with different hairstyles, hats, and skin tones; several cameras are pointed straight forward, creating the impression that they are photographing him all at once.

Back on the man, he keeps the black camera raised at chest height and grins toward the group, then turns back with an amused, friendly expression. The commercial ends on a dark blue-black title card with large white text. At the top it reads “$66*,” below that in much larger italic letters “Pronto!” and underneath, “A Polaroid Land camera.” At the bottom, smaller text explains the asterisk: “*Suggested list price.”

A dark Pizza Hut title card appears first, with the familiar red roof shape above large white “Pizza Hut” lettering. As the logo fades, it reveals an animated fantasy ballroom scene from Disney’s “Beauty and the Beast.” A golden chandelier glows overhead, pink streamers arch down from above, and rows of enchanted dishes and household objects line both sides like performers. In the center, animated castle servants dance: Cogsworth, the brown clock character with a round face and little clock hands, steps forward grinning; Mrs. Potts, the lavender-and-white teapot, and Chip, the small teacup, appear nearby; then Lumiere, the golden candlestick with candle-flame hands, takes center stage under the chandelier. The scene is bright, busy, and magical, with bubbles floating through the dark blue background and warm golden light sparkling around the characters.

The animation changes to a top-down view of Mrs. Potts on a polished reddish-brown floor, surrounded in a circle by six small golden candle-like figures with blue bands and glowing flames. She turns and smiles as the little flames form a ring around her. A small white copyright notice for Walt Disney appears in the lower right. The commercial then shows another animated castle interior: a tall pink wardrobe-like character with a smiling face stands at the left, while other enchanted household characters move around in a dim, grand hall with staircases, furniture, and warm shadows. A broom or feather-duster-like figure bobs up and down, and small teacup and footstool-like characters gather near the floor.

A close-up of the Beast follows. He is an animated, broad-faced, lion-like creature with brown fur, curved horns, thick eyebrows, fangs, and a white shirt under a dark cloak or jacket. He raises a large clawed hand toward the viewer, first with a mischievous grin and then with his mouth open wide, showing his teeth.

The Beast image dissolves into a real child’s play scene. A blond boy, about early elementary-school age, smiles warmly in a softly lit home setting. He holds up a Beast puppet beside his face. The puppet is a small figure of the Beast in a dark purple coat, white shirt front, and red lower garment; its furry head and raised hands resemble the animated character. The boy looks at it with delight, tilting his head slightly as if making the puppet perform.

The scene then shifts to a blond girl of similar age holding a Belle puppet. She wears a red top with a patterned vest or jumper over it. Belle’s puppet has brown hair and a bright yellow ball gown with a wide skirt. The girl gazes at the puppet fondly, smiling as she moves Belle closer to her face, as if admiring or playing with her.

A tabletop display presents two of the promotional puppets in the foreground: Cogsworth, the brown clock character with a golden face and hands raised, stands on the left, and Chip, the smiling teacup with a small chip in the rim, stands on the right. Large white text in the upper right reads “$1.49.” Along the bottom, small white text says, “One available each week while supplies last at participating restaurants.” Behind the tabletop, tall windowpanes show a dark blue night outside. In the background, the Belle and Beast puppets move into view and settle beside the others, so all four characters—Beast, Belle, Cogsworth, and Chip—are visible together, with Chip closest to the front right.

A “Beauty and the Beast” videocassette case appears upright on the tabletop. The cover art shows Belle in her yellow gown looking up at the Beast in a blue coat, with other enchanted characters below them. The title “Walt Disney Classic Beauty and the Beast” is printed prominently in gold and white on a purple-blue background. A hand enters from the right and pulls out a rectangular card from behind the cassette. The card has a red border, a Pizza Hut logo, and large black text reading “VALUE CARD,” with several smaller bullet-point offers printed beneath. The hand holds the card in front of the cassette so the Pizza Hut branding and “VALUE CARD” heading are clear. A wider product display then shows the videocassette standing behind the value card on the tabletop. White text along the bottom reads, “Full details inside cassette. Video cassette not available at Pizza Hut restaurants.”

The children appear together in the home setting, smiling and playing with the puppets. The boy holds the Beast puppet in front of him while the girl holds Belle. They bring the two figures close together as if acting out a scene. Both children look happy and absorbed, grinning as the puppets sway and face each other. The girl’s Belle puppet, with its shiny yellow skirt, moves in front of the boy’s Beast puppet, and the two children continue to animate them by hand.

The final images return to the romantic “Beauty and the Beast” world. The Beast and Belle figures appear side by side against a dark, theatrical background, then the scene becomes an animated ballroom. Belle, in her full golden gown, and the Beast, now in a royal blue coat, stand arm in arm beneath a huge glowing chandelier. They face away from the viewer and move together into an enormous golden hall lined with tall columns, arched windows, and draped balconies. The chandelier hangs low and luminous at the center, while the ceiling and walls shimmer with warm amber light, ending the commercial on the grand ballroom fantasy.

The commercial opens on a dark, studio-like surface divided into two choices. On the left sits a small, plain, round item wrapped tightly in white paper, like a fast-food burger. On the right is an open meatball sub on white deli paper, with a soft roll holding red-sauced meatballs and pale cheese. Above them, bold block text reads, “HEY, IT’S YOUR CHOICE,” with “YOUR” especially large in bright red. A small Subway logo appears near the sub.

The image cuts to a large price graphic: “96¢” in thick red numbers inside a jagged white starburst outlined in purple against a black background. The commercial then moves into appetizing food preparation images. A close view of the sandwich shows glossy red meatballs and marinara sauce resting over folded slices of white cheese inside bread; small fine print at the bottom says, “For a limited time on 6" subs.”

Inside a Subway kitchen or bakery area, a smiling young employee in a red Subway uniform shirt and matching red cap holds a metal tray loaded with long, pale sandwich rolls. She wears a thick gray oven mitt and stands in front of racks filled with freshly baked bread. She looks toward the viewer with a cheerful expression while presenting the tray.

Next, a large black pot of thick red marinara sauce is stirred with a wooden spoon. The sauce looks textured and chunky as the spoon moves through it. Fresh vegetables are then prepared in close detail: a ripe red tomato is sliced into even rounds with a knife, followed by a green bell pepper being cut into rings and strips, its seeds and pale inner ribs visible as the knife passes through.

The sandwich returns in an extended close view. The top half of the roll hovers over a row of round meatballs coated in red sauce, with melted-looking white cheese folded beneath them. The bread is light golden on the outside and soft, airy white inside. Sauce gathers along the bottom edge and around the meatballs, making the sub look full and messy in a deliberate, appetizing way.

After the sandwich close-up, the plain wrapped burger appears alone on the dark surface, small and unadorned, casting a shadow. The scene widens back into a comparison layout, with the wrapped burger on the left and bold promotional text and the sandwich area emerging on the right.

The final screen is a full product card. Large stacked text reads, “THE SUBWAY MEATBALL SUB” in orange and red, then “JUST 96¢ FOR A LIMITED TIME” in bright yellow-green. To the right is the meatball sub again, partly wrapped in white paper, with red-sauced meatballs spilling visibly from the roll. The Subway logo sits beneath the sandwich, completing the offer display.

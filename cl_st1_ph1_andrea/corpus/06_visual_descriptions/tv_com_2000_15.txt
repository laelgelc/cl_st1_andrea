Against a clean white background, animated peanuts and peanut shells drift and tumble through the air. Pale tan peanut kernels separate from their rough, beige shells and begin dropping into a rounded brown chocolate cup, like the hollow base of a Hershey’s Kiss. More peanut pieces fall inside until the opening is filled with smooth, golden peanut shapes. Around the cup, broken peanut shells lie scattered on the white surface. A white paper plume with the blue word “KISSES” curls upward from behind it, and a pointed chocolate top lowers into place, transforming the rounded cup into the familiar teardrop shape of a Hershey’s Kiss.

The completed chocolate Kiss sits among the peanut shells, with its white “KISSES” ribbon waving above it. It rocks and bounces lightly from side to side, almost as if dancing. The chocolate shape leans, pivots, and lifts slightly off the surface while the ribbon trails behind in a loose curve. The top then rises away, revealing the inside: a thick, smooth, tan peanut butter filling swirled neatly within the brown chocolate rim. The view lingers on the creamy spiral, showing a glossy, soft-looking peak at the center, before the chocolate top returns and the Kiss appears whole again with its ribbon curling behind.

The Kiss separates once more, the pointed chocolate cap floating to one side while the rounded base remains, its peanut butter center visible at the top. A square of shiny silver foil appears on the white surface nearby. The rounded filled base rolls and tips onto the foil, while the pointed chocolate cap hovers and spins around it. The blue “KISSES” ribbon flips and loops in the air as the pieces move together over the foil. The foil begins to gather and crinkle upward around the chocolate, enclosing it. By the end, the candy is wrapped in reflective silver foil with red printed markings, shaped into a small Hershey’s Kiss, with the paper plume sticking out from the top.

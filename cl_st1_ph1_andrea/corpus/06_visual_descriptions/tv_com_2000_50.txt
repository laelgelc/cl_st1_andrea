Against a bright yellow studio background, the commercial opens in an extreme close view of a small, clear plastic device with red-orange parts inside. The white Energizer name is partly visible across the transparent casing. Inside the round, ridged dispenser are several tiny silver button batteries arranged in a circle. A red, thumb-shaped control and a small shiny metal cylinder at the tip dominate the foreground, making the mechanism look enlarged and precise.

A beige plastic model of an ear and hearing aid comes into view on the left. The clear dispenser is held from the right and brought toward the small hearing aid battery opening. A single round silver battery sits at the end of the dispenser’s short arm, held flat and steady. The red arm lines the battery up with the tiny compartment on the hearing aid. The battery is guided into place at the edge of the beige hearing aid shell, the red arm fitting very close to the small opening. The action is shown in a highly magnified way, emphasizing that the battery is being placed accurately without fingers having to pinch it directly.

The product packaging then fills the screen. It is a blister-pack card with a black top band and large white “Energizer” lettering. Beside it, the name “EZChange” appears, with “EZ” in a yellow box and “Change” in black. The package reads “Hearing Aid Batteries,” and an orange square at the lower left shows the size number “13.” A red circular badge on the right says, “Precise Placement EVERY TIME!” In the clear blister area, the round transparent dispenser is visible, with its red thumb control mounted over the circular battery holder.

Two fingers hold the package upright against the yellow background, then the printed card fades away visually, leaving the actual round dispenser held between fingertips. The dispenser is small, clear, and puck-like, with scalloped edges and a ring of silver hearing aid batteries visible inside. A red control sits on the front like a small lever or dial.

A close view shows a finger turning or pressing the red control on the dispenser. The batteries inside shift around the circular track. The red control is moved until one battery reaches the small exit point at the edge. The tiny silver battery slides out to the end of a short clear arm, where it sits neatly on a black-rimmed magnetic tip. The battery remains flat and centered, held securely at the end of the arm.

The dispenser, still held between fingers, is brought toward a small beige hearing aid held in another hand. The hearing aid has a tiny open battery door or compartment. The dispenser’s magnetic arm carries the silver battery forward and lines it up with the opening. The arm touches the hearing aid, the battery is placed directly into the compartment, and then the dispenser is pulled away to the right, leaving the battery seated on the hearing aid. The fingers holding the hearing aid remain steady on the left, showing the battery in place without being handled directly.

In the upper panel, the dispenser places a battery into a small red hearing aid. In the lower left panel, a white behind-the-ear style hearing aid is shown with the round battery held at the dispenser’s tip. In the lower right panel, the dispenser aligns a battery with another small beige hearing aid. The panels simplify the demonstration: different hearing aid shapes appear, but the same magnetic arm holds and places the battery.

Finally, several Energizer EZChange hearing aid battery packages stand upright in a row on the yellow surface. Each package has the black Energizer header, the EZChange branding, and a visible colored dispenser or battery holder in the blister area, suggesting different battery sizes or styles. The Energizer Bunny appears in front of the packages: a small pink toy rabbit with tall ears, sunglasses, and a drum strapped to its front. At the bottom left, black italic text appears: “It changes batteries …” The bunny continues off toward the right edge while the row of Energizer EZChange packages remains displayed behind it.

A woman with very short hair, hoop earrings, and a gray button-up shirt stands beside tall white curtains in a bright room. Warm sunlight pours in as she pulls the curtain aside and smiles toward the window, enjoying the light. She then moves around an empty room with high white walls, wood flooring, tall shuttered windows, and a white door. Holding a turquoise-cased smartphone in one hand, she looks upward and around the space as if assessing what needs to be done. She reaches out and runs her hand along the blank white wall, then brings up the phone and taps the screen.

The phone display fills the view with app icons, including an orange square icon with The Home Depot logo. The orange Home Depot screen appears, followed by a page with bold text reading, “DON’T GUESSTIMATE. CALCULATE.” Smaller text underneath says, “Know exactly how much you need at every step of your project,” though parts of it blur as the phone moves. The app then shows a Home Depot “Paint Calculator” page. The visible form includes “Room Size” and fields such as height, width, and length, suggesting she is calculating how much paint she needs.

Back in the room, the woman sets up a silver step ladder with a large yellow tray at the top. She smiles while working around the ladder. On one of the ladder steps sits a black can labeled “BEHR MARQUEE,” with painting supplies and a roller tray above it. The previously white wall is now partly covered in a warm orange-brown paint. With a paint roller on an extension handle, she rolls color onto the wall in broad strokes near the ladder, filling in the area evenly.

The scene shifts to another phone held horizontally in two hands. On the screen is a Home Depot video page with a large play button and visible text: “HOW TO INSTALL Hardwood Flooring.” A finger taps the play button. A bearded adult man with dark hair, wearing a green top and jeans, kneels on a wood floor in a home interior near a front door. He works carefully beside an open flooring box and a dark underlayment sheet, placing light wood-look planks into position. He presses a plank flat with his hands, smiles down at his work, and then uses a small yellow-and-black rubber mallet to tap a piece into place, aligning the flooring.

A phone screen then shows a Home Depot delivery option. A tile with a small truck icon reads “Scheduled Delivery,” and a finger presses it; the tile turns orange to indicate it has been selected. Outside a house, a Home Depot delivery worker wearing a dark cap, dark shirt, khaki pants, and a white face mask carries a cardboard box. The box has The Home Depot logo and the printed slogan, “How doers get more done.” Other Home Depot boxes are stacked inside the open back of a delivery truck, whose side also bears the orange logo. The worker brings the box to a front porch with a wooden door, blue shutters, potted plants, and a white rocking chair.

A woman in a pink top and blue jeans steps forward and picks up the delivered Home Depot box. From inside the open box, the woman and a young girl look down into it. The girl, with long braided hair and a striped shirt, watches as the woman reaches in and lifts out packaged parts.

In a bathroom with bright windows, a white sink, and a large mirror, the woman begins installing a shiny chrome faucet. Seen partly through reflections, she leans over the sink, focused and smiling slightly as she positions the fixture. A product box nearby is labeled “Rubicon” and “THE BOLD LOOK OF KOHLER,” indicating a Kohler faucet. She steadies the faucet body with one hand and adjusts it into place on the sink.

The woman who was painting earlier reappears in the sunlit room, now with the wall fully painted a rich orange-brown. She pulls up and drags away a large section of old carpet or padding, exposing the wood floor beneath. The material billows as she yanks it across the room, then collapses toward the floor as she continues clearing the space.

In the finished bathroom, the woman in the pink top and the young girl stand together at the white sink. Both smile and laugh as the girl reaches toward the new chrome faucet, apparently testing the water. Their reflections are visible in the mirror, along with countertop accessories such as a soap dispenser and toothbrush holders. The commercial ends on a clean white screen with a small orange Home Depot logo centered.

## Interpretation of Factor f5 positive pole

Factor **f5 positive** appears to encode a discourse of **domestic and bodily cleanliness framed through softness, beauty, gentleness, whiteness, and feminine care**. The pole is strongly associated with commercials for **soap, skin care, hair care, laundry detergent, bleach, diapers, and household washing products**, especially where the verbal pitch links cleaning performance with sensory or aesthetic qualities such as *soft*, *gentle*, *lovely*, *beautiful*, *bright*, and *white*.

### Possible labels for this pole

1. **“Gentle Beauty and Domestic Cleanliness”**  
2. **“Soft, Feminine Clean-Care Discourse”**  
3. **“Beauty, Softness, and Laundry Whiteness”**  
4. **“Domestic Hygiene as Beauty and Gentleness”**  
5. **“Clean, Soft, Bright: Personal and Household Care”**

The strongest label is probably:

> **Gentle Beauty and Domestic Cleanliness**

This captures both major lexical fields: personal-care beauty/softness and household/laundry cleaning.

## Justification from the factor loadings

The loading words form several overlapping semantic clusters.

### 1. Beauty, femininity, and softness

High-loading terms include:

- **lovely** 1.09  
- **beauty** 1.02  
- **gentle** .89  
- **woman** .83  
- **delicate** .81  
- **soft** .65  
- **lady** .63  
- **beautiful** .41  
- **girl** .39  
- **young** .33  
- **creamy** .31  

These words point to a discourse in which products are promoted through ideals of **feminine attractiveness, delicacy, youthfulness, softness, and beauty**. The pole is not merely about cleanliness in a technical sense; it is cleanliness as something that makes skin, hair, clothes, or the self feel *lovely*, *soft*, *gentle*, and *beautiful*.

### 2. Soap, washing, and skin/body care

Another strong cluster concerns bathing and personal cleansing:

- **soap** .89  
- **suds** .63  
- **water** .31  
- **ingredient** .35  
- **creamy** .31  

These terms suggest commercials for soaps, cleansing bars, deodorant soaps, and skin-care products. The discourse often presents soap not just as a cleaner but as a product that protects skin, avoids dryness, moisturizes, or produces a pleasant bodily feeling.

### 3. Laundry, washing, and whiteness

A second major product field is laundry and household washing:

- **wash** .72  
- **detergent** .66  
- **clothes** .61  
- **machine** .60  
- **white** .56  
- **bright** .41  
- **dirty** .32  
- **wear** .37  
- **black** .34  
- **green** .39  

This cluster points to laundry commercials emphasizing **clean clothes, whiteness, brightness, stain removal, colour protection, and washing-machine contexts**. The words *white* and *bright* are especially important because they connect cleaning to visible aesthetic improvement.

### 4. Domestic-product vocabulary

Terms such as **woman**, **lady**, **clothes**, **wash**, **detergent**, **machine**, **suds**, and **soap** suggest a domestic-consumer environment. The pole reflects commercials that address household care, laundry, bathing, beauty routines, and family care.

## Justification from the example excerpts

The high-scoring excerpts strongly support this interpretation. Most come from **1980s commercials** and are concentrated in personal-care and laundry-cleaning categories.

### Dove: beauty, women, softness, skin care

The Dove excerpt contains the loading words **soap**, **woman**, and **soft**:

> “We asked women to give up their regular soap and take the Dove seven-day test… With Dove, I can't get over how smooth my skin feels… It can give you softer, smoother skin…”

This is a clear example of the pole’s discourse: cleansing is framed as **skin improvement**, **softness**, and **feeling good about oneself**. The product is not simply a soap; it is a beauty-care solution.

### Lysol Laundry Sanitizer: wash, detergent, whiteness

The Lysol excerpt contains **wash**, **detergent**, and **white**:

> “Just when you thought your wash was clean, now your wash can be really clean… works with your detergent… leaves your wash so white, bright, fresh-smelling…”

Here the discourse shifts from bodily softness to laundry hygiene, but the same general pole remains: cleanliness is intensified through **whiteness**, **freshness**, and visible improvement.

### Clorox 2: clothes, colours, whites, brightness

The Clorox excerpt contains **clothes**, **white**, and **green**:

> “Mama keeps green, green like a dragon. Mama keeps white, bright like a wish come true… helps clothes look newer longer… colors and whites clean, bright and pretty.”

This example is especially revealing because it combines laundry care with aesthetic language: clothes are not only clean but **bright**, **pretty**, and **new-looking**. The domestic role of “Mama” also connects the pole to household-care discourse.

### Tide with bleach: detergent and whiteness

The Tide excerpt contains **woman**, **detergent**, and **white**:

> “Tide with bleach whitens better than any other detergent… gets gleaming white…”

Again, the focal promise is visible cleaning performance, especially **whiteness**. The presence of **woman** also reflects the gendered domestic framing common in this pole.

### Irish Spring and Zest: soap, freshness, clean feeling

The Irish Spring and Zest examples foreground **soap**, freshness, deodorizing, rinsing, and feeling clean:

> “Irish Spring's green and white stripes have two truly effective deodorants…”

> “Zest is different from soap… rinse without leaving sticky film… Nothing comes between me and feeling so clean.”

These examples show the personal hygiene side of the dimension: soap is evaluated through **freshness**, **clean feeling**, and bodily confidence.

### Huggies: soft and gentle care

The Huggies example contains **gentle** and **soft**:

> “Huggies diapers are form-fitting… with soft, gentle elastic at the leg…”

Although this is not a beauty or laundry ad, it fits the broader pole through the language of **softness**, **gentleness**, and care for the body. It shows that the factor is not limited to adult beauty products; it includes products that promise mild, protective, comfortable care.

## Decades driving the pole

The decade scores show that the **1980s** are the decade most associated with this positive pole:

| Decade | Mean f5 score |
|---|---:|
| 1950s | -0.262 |
| 1960s | -0.650 |
| 1970s | -0.262 |
| 1980s | **0.019** |
| 1990s | -0.220 |
| 2000s | -0.294 |
| 2010s | -0.340 |
| 2020s | -0.223 |

The 1980s have the highest mean score and are the only decade with a slightly positive mean. Moreover, all the supplied high-scoring examples come from the **1980s**, which strongly suggests that this pole is driven by a cluster of 1980s commercials for soaps, detergents, bleach products, diapers, and beauty/hygiene products.

However, the 1980s mean is only slightly above zero. This means the pole should not be interpreted as representing all 1980s advertising. Rather, it identifies a **specific 1980s-heavy discourse cluster** within the verbal subcorpus: commercials that combine household cleaning, personal hygiene, beauty, softness, and visible cleanliness.

---

## Diachronic tendency

Diachronically, this pole appears to peak in the **1980s**, after lower scores in the 1950s–1970s, especially the 1960s. After the 1980s, the mean scores fall back into negative territory in the 1990s, 2000s, 2010s, and 2020s.

This suggests that the discourse of **soft/gentle beauty and domestic cleanliness**, at least as captured by these lexical loadings, is most characteristic of the selected **1980s verbal commercials**. The pole may reflect the prominence in that decade of verbal advertising for laundry detergents, bleach, soaps, deodorant soaps, shampoos, skin-care bars, and baby-care products, where the pitch relied heavily on words like *soft*, *gentle*, *white*, *bright*, *clean*, *soap*, *wash*, and *detergent*.

---

## Summary interpretation

Factor **f5 positive** represents a discourse of **clean-care aesthetics**: products are sold by promising not only functional cleaning but also **softness, gentleness, beauty, freshness, brightness, whiteness, and domestic care**. The pole brings together personal hygiene and household washing, especially soaps, detergents, bleaches, shampoos, skin-care products, and baby-care products. It is most strongly driven by the **1980s** commercials in the sample.

A concise label would be:

> **Gentle Beauty and Domestic Cleanliness**

Alternative labels include:

- **Soft, Feminine Clean-Care Discourse**
- **Beauty, Softness, and Laundry Whiteness**
- **Domestic Hygiene as Beauty and Gentleness**
- **Clean, Soft, Bright: Personal and Household Care**

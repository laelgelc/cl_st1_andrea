## Factor f7 (negative pole): interpretation

The negative pole of Factor 7 appears to encode a discourse of **domestic/lifestyle product utility, protection, and everyday enjoyment**, with a particularly recent-commercial inflection around **staying safe, staying connected, pets/family, and practical problem-solving**.

This pole is driven most strongly by the **2020s**, which have the lowest mean score on Factor 7:

| Decade | Mean fac7 |
|---|---:|
| 2020s | 0.058 |
| 2010s | 0.087 |
| 1990s | 0.120 |
| 2000s | 0.127 |
| 1980s | 0.146 |
| 1970s | 0.214 |
| 1960s | 0.495 |
| 1950s | 0.553 |

Because this is the **negative pole**, the relevant diachronic tendency is the gradual movement downward across time, with the 2020s closest to this pole. However, all decade means remain positive, so the pole should be interpreted as a **relative tendency in recent high-scoring individual texts**, not as the dominant average discourse of an entire decade.

## Main lexical pattern

The loading words cluster around several related semantic fields.

### 1. Everyday product appeal and promotional evaluation

High negative loadings include:

- **plenty**  
- **special**  
- **favorite**  
- **wonderful**  
- **enjoy**  
- **pleasure**  
- **offer**  
- **product**  
- **hurry**  
- **visit**

These are recognisably commercial words. They frame the advertised item as desirable, enjoyable, available, and worth acting on. This part of the pole reflects a general advertising register of **positive product presentation and consumer invitation**.

### 2. Food, flavour, and domestic consumption

Several loadings point to food, taste, and household routines:

- **food**
- **flavor**
- **rich**
- **breakfast**
- **dish**
- **prepare**
- **suds**

These suggest a discourse of **domestic consumption**, including meals, taste, cleaning, and household care. The words are not limited to food advertising but evoke the everyday home sphere: preparing, eating, washing, enjoying, and maintaining.

### 3. Family, pets, safety, and care

Another important lexical set includes:

- **young**
- **child**
- **dog**
- **safe**
- **stay**
- **lose**
- **weight**
- **build**

This cluster introduces concerns with bodies, families, children, pets, safety, and wellbeing. The pole therefore also has a protective or care-oriented quality: keeping people, pets, bodies, homes, or routines in a desirable state.

### 4. Social interaction and action

Words such as:

- **talk**
- **visit**
- **act**
- **lead**
- **stay**

suggest commercials organised around interpersonal action, advice, invitation, or social positioning. They imply a consumer being prompted to do something, maintain something, or participate in something.

## Evidence from the excerpts

The excerpts are mostly from the **2020s**, which is consistent with the decade scores. They activate only a small subset of the loading vocabulary, especially **stay** and **dog**, so the examples illustrate only part of the pole. Nevertheless, they show a coherent recent-commercial version of the discourse.

### “Stay” as maintenance, protection, and connection

Several 2020s examples contain **stay**, and the word is used in different but related ways.

In the Garnier hair-care commercial:

> “Garnier presents Ferti Sleek and Stay Serum… For ultra glassy sleek that stays up to five washes.”

Here **stay** refers to product durability: the desired effect remains over time. The discourse is one of **product efficacy and maintained appearance**.

In the Taco Bell example:

> “Why stay inside the lines when you could add some flavor to everything around you?”

Here **stay** is used metaphorically, positioning the consumer as someone who can break routine and add flavour. This links the pole’s vocabulary of **stay** and **flavor** to a lifestyle discourse of playful consumption.

In the Hyundai commercial:

> “life's full of little accidents and the hyundai tucson helps make sure they stay little…”

Here **stay** belongs to a safety discourse. The product prevents small problems from becoming serious ones. This aligns with the loading word **safe** and the wider care/protection cluster.

In the Microsoft Viva example:

> “continue to stay connected as a team.”

Here **stay** is about workplace connection and continuity. The product is presented as maintaining social and organisational cohesion.

Across these examples, **stay** indexes a broad commercial promise: the product helps desirable conditions continue — sleek hair, safe driving, connected teams, manageable accidents, or expanded lifestyle possibilities.

### “Dog” as pet/family care and everyday domestic life

Several examples feature **dog**, another loading word.

The Ring commercial says:

> “Pets are family… Search Party from Ring uses AI to help families find lost dogs.”

This strongly activates the care/protection side of the pole. The product is framed through family, loss, neighbourhood, safety, and reunion.

The Spruce commercial says:

> “Your dog is bored, trapped inside because of your weed killer. But now, there's Spruce, the pet-friendly weed and grass killer.”

Here the product solves a domestic safety problem: weed killer must be effective but also pet-friendly. Again, the discourse is one of **home maintenance plus care for vulnerable family members/pets**.

The Red Bull example uses a dog in a comic narrative:

> “Charlie… you crazy silly dog… Red Bull gives you wings.”

This is less directly protective, but it still locates the commercial in an everyday, informal, domestic interaction involving a pet.

Together, the dog examples suggest that this pole includes a contemporary commercial tendency to humanise products through pets, family relations, neighbourhoods, and ordinary home situations.

## Diachronic tendency

The decade means show a steady downward movement from the 1950s and 1960s toward the 2020s. Since the negative pole is associated with the lowest scores, the pole is most relevant to the **2020s**, with the **2010s, 1990s, and 2000s** also relatively closer than earlier decades.

This suggests that the discourse has become more prominent in recent commercial speech, especially in forms involving:

- products embedded in everyday lifestyle scenarios;
- safety and risk management;
- family and pet care;
- maintaining desired states;
- convenience and practical problem-solving;
- soft lifestyle appeals rather than overtly elaborate sales talk.

The examples especially point to a 2020s style in which commercials sell products by showing how they help consumers **stay safe, stay connected, keep pets safe, preserve appearance, manage domestic life, or solve small everyday problems**.

---

## Possible labels for f7 negative pole

### 1. **Everyday Care and Lifestyle Utility**

This is perhaps the strongest label. It captures the combination of household routines, pets, family, safety, food/flavour, product usefulness, and maintained wellbeing. The loading words point to everyday consumer domains, while the examples show products helping with hair care, pet safety, vehicle safety, workplace connection, and home maintenance.

### 2. **Domestic Product Care and Protection**

This label foregrounds the domestic-care aspect of the pole. It fits loadings such as **dish, food, breakfast, suds, child, dog, safe, stay**, and it fits examples involving dogs, weed killer, lost pets, family safety, and accident prevention. It is slightly narrower than the full lexical pattern because it underplays the promotional words such as **special, offer, favorite, wonderful, enjoy**.

### 3. **Maintained Wellbeing and Practical Consumer Solutions**

This label is motivated especially by the examples. Products are represented as helping desirable states continue: hair stays sleek, accidents stay little, teams stay connected, dogs stay safe or are found, homes remain usable. It captures the repeated **stay** pattern and the pragmatic problem-solving orientation of many excerpts.

### 4. **Home, Family, and Enjoyment-Oriented Advertising**

This broader label reflects the loadings around **food, flavor, breakfast, dish, child, dog, enjoy, pleasure, favorite, wonderful**. It captures both the domestic and positive-affective sides of the pole. However, it is somewhat less precise for the 2020s examples involving AI, cars, Microsoft Viva, and safety technologies.

---

## Recommended label

A suitable concise label for this pole would be:

> **Everyday Care and Lifestyle Utility**

This label best balances the factor loadings and the excerpts. The loadings show a broad commercial vocabulary of domestic products, enjoyment, food, safety, family, and promotional appeal. The examples, especially from the 2020s, show this discourse realised through practical scenarios of staying safe, staying connected, protecting pets, maintaining appearance, and solving everyday problems.

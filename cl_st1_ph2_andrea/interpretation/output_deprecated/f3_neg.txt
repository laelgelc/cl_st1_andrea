## Interpretation of Factor 3 negative pole

Factor 3 negative appears to encode a discourse of **everyday domestic consumption**, centred on **home routines, family-oriented products, food/drink, cleaning, and convenience**. The pole brings together the verbal world of the kitchen, breakfast table, laundry/sink area, and household product demonstration, with a strong emphasis on products as **handy, pleasant, sweet, bright, soft, rich, and easy to use**.

Although the examples are mostly from the 2000s and include energetic youth-oriented cereal/candy advertising, the loading profile is broader: it points to a recurrent commercial discourse of **domestic usefulness and sensory enjoyment**.

### Lexical evidence

The strongest loading words cluster into several related semantic fields:

1. **Family and domestic roles**
   - *mother* is the strongest loading item, with *girl*, *everybody*, and *dog* also contributing.
   - These terms suggest a household/family setting rather than an abstract corporate or prestige-oriented discourse.

2. **Kitchen, meals, and food/drink consumption**
   - *breakfast*, *dinner*, *drink*, *serve*, *mmm*, *sweet*, *rich*, *golden*, *white*.
   - This cluster evokes cereal, drinks, meals, desserts, and taste-based product appeal. The interjection *mmm* is especially associated with sensory pleasure and appetite.

3. **Cleaning, washing, and household maintenance**
   - *sink*, *detergent*, *wash*, *suds*, *paper*, *box*.
   - These terms point to laundry, dishwashing, cleaning products, and practical domestic goods.

4. **Convenience and positive product qualities**
   - *handy*, *wonderful*, *soft*, *bright*, *electric*.
   - These items indicate a discourse of household ease: products are framed as useful, efficient, pleasant, modern, or labour-saving.

Taken together, the loadings suggest a commercial register built around **ordinary household needs**: feeding the family, serving meals, washing, cleaning, and making domestic life easier or more enjoyable.

### Evidence from the excerpts

The high-scoring examples at this pole, especially from the 2000s, illustrate several parts of the loading pattern.

- The Honeycomb cereal excerpt contains *mmm* and *breakfast*:  
  > “Mmm, that high-sweet crunch can’t get past me… Honeycomb cereal is part of this good breakfast.”  
  This strongly reflects the food/taste/breakfast component of the pole: sensory enjoyment, sweetness, crunch, and the formulaic “part of this good breakfast” claim.

- The Pop-Tarts excerpt repeatedly uses *breakfast*:  
  > “We’re the breakfast crew… Part of this complete breakfast… breakfast should be loud.”  
  Here the domestic meal setting is transformed into a playful, youth-oriented, high-energy style, but the core discourse remains centred on breakfast foods and packaged consumption.

- The Baby Bottle Pop excerpt uses *drink*:  
  > “a great tasting baby bottle pop that you can drink…”  
  This supports the sweet/snack/drink component of the pole.

- The detergent-tablet excerpt contains *handy*:  
  > “these handy little tablets can tackle more grass stains too.”  
  This illustrates the convenience-cleaning side of the dimension: a product is promoted as compact, practical, and effective against household stains.

- The Pontiac excerpt uses *everybody*:  
  > “Everybody everywhere… It’s getting around fast.”  
  This is less domestic, but it matches the pole’s more general mass-consumer inclusiveness: products are presented as widely accessible, everyday, and for “everybody.”

Some later excerpts listed for this pole have few or no explicit loading words, but they remain thematically adjacent in places: for example, the Electrosol and Dawn examples involve dishes, rinsing, stains, cleaning, foam, grease, and ease of use. Even without the exact loading words, they are consistent with the household-cleaning/convenience discourse suggested by the loadings.

### Decades driving the pole

For a negative pole, the relevant decades are those with the lowest mean Factor 3 scores. Here, the lowest means are:

- **2000s: 0.225**
- **1990s: 0.240**
- **2020s: 0.291**
- **1980s: 0.311**
- **2010s: 0.340**

The **2000s** are especially important because all the supplied high-scoring examples come from that decade. This suggests that, in the selected commercial verbal subcorpus, the 2000s particularly activate this pole through commercials for cereals, candy drinks, cleaning tablets, dishwashing products, and mass-market consumer goods.

Diachronically, the pole appears to be relatively more characteristic of **late twentieth- and early twenty-first-century commercials**, especially the **2000s**, rather than the earliest decades. However, because all decade means are positive rather than strongly negative, this should be interpreted as a **relative tendency** rather than a complete decade-specific separation.

### Suggested labels for Factor 3 negative

Possible labels include:

1. **Domestic Convenience and Everyday Consumption**
   - Best overall label. It captures the household, cleaning, food, family, and ease-of-use vocabulary.

2. **Household Routines, Food, and Cleaning Products**
   - More descriptive and concrete. This label foregrounds the main product domains represented by the loadings: breakfast, dinner, drinks, detergent, washing, sink, suds.

3. **Family-Home Product Practicality**
   - Emphasises the family/domestic role terms such as *mother*, *girl*, *everybody*, together with practical adjectives such as *handy*.

4. **Kitchen, Laundry, and Mealtime Consumer Discourse**
   - A narrower label that highlights the domestic spaces implied by *sink*, *wash*, *detergent*, *breakfast*, *dinner*, *serve*, and *drink*.

5. **Sensory Domestic Product Appeal**
   - Useful if the emphasis is placed on the evaluative and sensory terms: *mmm*, *sweet*, *rich*, *golden*, *soft*, *bright*, *wonderful*.

The strongest label is probably **“Domestic Convenience and Everyday Consumption”**, because it accommodates both the full loading pattern and the example excerpts: the pole is not only about food, nor only about cleaning, but about ordinary consumer goods embedded in domestic routines and promoted through convenience, sensory pleasure, and broad household appeal.

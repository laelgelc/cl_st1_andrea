A man in a suit first appears in front of a large backdrop or sign with the word “Kellogg’s” in script and the phrase “greatest name in cereals” partly visible beneath it. Behind him is a stadium-like or arena setting with rows of seats, giving the opening a sponsored-program feel before the Dixie cup scene begins.

The commercial then moves into a black-and-white kitchen set. An adult woman with very light, shoulder-length, curled hair stands beside a rounded 1950s-style refrigerator. She wears a shiny, sequined performance-style outfit with puffed sleeves, a fitted bodice, a short flared skirt, and a dark scarf or chest panel with lettering on it. She smiles toward the viewer and gestures with one hand while standing between a curtained window with venetian blinds on the left and the refrigerator and wall cabinets on the right.

She turns to the refrigerator, places one hand on the door, and opens it. Bending forward, she reaches into the lower part of the refrigerator. The inside shelves hold several containers, including a pitcher and small boxes or cartons. She takes out a glass pitcher filled with a light-colored drink and also brings out a small paper cup. Standing upright again, she holds the pitcher in one hand and the cup in the other, presenting them in front of her as she speaks.

Facing forward, she pours the drink from the pitcher into the paper cup. The cup is small, tapered, and light-toned, with a decorative band and the Dixie name visible in later close-ups. She looks down briefly as she pours, then raises the filled cup. After holding it up for the viewer, she brings it to her mouth and drinks. Her expression is pleased and confident; after sipping, she lowers the cup and continues addressing the viewer, holding the Dixie cup near her chest.

A close-up then isolates a Dixie cup in a wall-mounted dispenser. The dispenser is a vertical holder with a stack of paper cups inside; the bottom cup protrudes downward, ready to be pulled out. The visible cup has a patterned band around it and the word “Dixie” printed on the front. Over this product close-up, large block letters appear near the bottom reading “NO GLASSES TO WASH.” The same dispenser and cup remain centered as the message changes. A hand reaches in from the right and pulls a cup from the dispenser while the words “NO BROKEN GLASSES” appear across the lower part of the picture. The close-up continues with the cup and dispenser as another bold caption appears: “MORE SANITARY.”

The woman reappears in the kitchen, now standing near the wall-mounted dispenser beside the refrigerator. She faces forward, then turns her head toward the dispenser and gestures to it with a smile, visually linking the cup she has been using with the handy dispenser on the wall. She continues smiling and speaking directly to the viewer, with the dispenser clearly visible over her shoulder.

The scene changes to a store or pantry shelf stocked with Dixie products. On the left are several boxed “Dixie Dispenser” packages displayed upright in a cardboard tray. On the right is a row of tall cylindrical refill boxes labeled “DIXIE CUPS,” arranged tightly side by side. A hand reaches in from above and selects one of the tall refill containers, pulling it slightly forward from the row so its label is prominent. The arrangement emphasizes both the dispenser packages and the refill boxes as items to buy together.

The commercial ends on a simple title card with large script and serif lettering: “Dixie Cup” and below it, “America’s No. 1 Paper Cup.”

The commercial uses simple black-and-white cutout-style animation on a plain gray background. It begins with an empty gray field, then a large white numeral “1” appears in the center, emphasizing a single way or single choice. A round white animated shape enters beside it, like a flat disk with a pie-shaped slit cut from one side for a mouth. It has no eyes or limbs, but the opening and closing slit makes it read as a “taster” or mouth-like character.

A small jar of Skippy appears to the right of the round mouth character. The jar is drawn in white with a dark rectangular label reading “SKIPPY.” The mouth character faces the jar, opening toward it. The jar’s lid separates upward, and the label area seems to loosen. The word “SKIPPY” pulls out from the jar toward the mouth, as if the mouth is trying to take in the name itself. The jar pieces then spread apart and rearrange visually: the top lid floats above, the lower jar shape remains below, and the mouth character sits inside a dark rectangular panel framed by white, making the arrangement look like a television screen.

Inside this television-like frame, the round mouth opens toward the right. A white jagged wavy line emerges from the mouth and travels across the gray background, like a visible sound wave. At the far right, a plain white oval appears, resembling an ear or listening organ. The wavy line runs from the mouth toward and across this oval, continuing through it. The visual idea is that the spoken message or sound waves can reach the ear-like shape, but the ear remains just a passive oval while the mouth stays inside the TV-shaped frame.

The TV-like frame slides partly out of view to the left, leaving the wavy line stretched across the screen and the oval ear-like shape near the center. The jagged line continues across and around the oval, passing through it without changing it. Then the image shifts to a simple drawing of an eye: a white almond-shaped outline with a dark oval pupil in the middle. The eye grows more detailed, with concentric rings in the center like a target or iris, reinforcing the idea of looking.

The eye gives way to a rounded central shape containing a light bulb outline, with two narrow oval shapes on either side. The bulb becomes the main object, enlarged against the gray field. It has a round top, a short striped screw base, and a small pull chain made of tiny dots hanging along one side. A dark crossed-out symbol appears inside the bulb: a small container-like shape with a drop beneath it is marked over with a bold X, suggesting a rejected thought or objection. The light bulb remains plain and still, representing thinking rather than tasting.

As it lowers, the word “SKIPPY” becomes visible on the panel, along with a large white question mark to the left. The panel covers most of the bulb, leaving only the base and dangling chain briefly visible beneath it. The panel then hangs centered with the large “?” and “SKIPPY” written across it.

The character opens its wedge-shaped mouth toward the word and takes it in, as though actually tasting the Skippy name rather than merely hearing or seeing it. Once the word disappears, the round character remains alone on the gray background. Its mouth changes into a curved smile-like line on the lower right side, giving it a satisfied expression.

Then the brand name “SKIPPY” is spelled across the top of the screen, each letter printed on a separate small jar or can-shaped tile. The smiling round character sits below the row of letters, still simple and featureless except for the curved smile. The image briefly dims, then a large white heart appears centered on the gray background. Finally, a jar of the product appears in the middle of the heart. Its dark label clearly reads “SKIPPY PEANUT BUTTER,” presenting the package as the final visual focus.

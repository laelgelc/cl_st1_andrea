The commercial opens in black and white with a simple animated television set floating against a plain light background. The set is drawn as a boxy 1950s tabletop TV with a rounded screen, two small knobs below, and a face on the screen. The face looks worried and unwell, with uneven eyes, raised brows, a drooping mouth, and a long cartoon nose. As the sequence continues, the television’s face grows more strained: its eyes squint and dark shadows spread over the screen until the picture appears almost blacked out, suggesting the set’s picture tube has failed.

The front screen section then pulls away from the television like a rounded glass panel being removed. The dark, smoky-looking old tube moves off to the left, while a clean, bright replacement tube slides in from the right. The new rounded tube is pale and luminous, and it fits into the front of the TV cabinet. Once in place, light rays burst outward around the screen. The face on the television returns, first calm and then smiling broadly, as if the set has been revived. The letters “RCA” appear near the happy face, connecting the restored picture with the brand.

The scene shifts to a dark, starry, sparkling background. Large white capital letters appear one phrase at a time, building into the message: “MORE RCA PICTURE TUBES NOW IN USE.” At the bottom, smaller text begins with “…than any,” continuing the comparison implied by the narration. The typography is bold, white, and centered, standing out against the glittering dark field.

A simplified technical drawing of a picture tube appears next. A thin line runs from the gun toward the screen, ending in a small bright point, visually illustrating how the tube produces detail on the screen. The word “SHARPER” appears above the diagram in large white capital letters. The diagram continues to show lines traveling from the electron gun toward the screen, and the word “BRIGHTER” is added beneath “SHARPER.” The message then becomes an equation: “SHARPER + BRIGHTER = CLEARER,” with the tube diagram still visible beside it.

The same technical drawing remains on screen as the wording changes to “LAST LONGER.” The tube’s screen and internal lines stay visible, with the RCA logo below, presenting the product as a precise internal component rather than just an outside television set.

The diagram then transforms into a more three-dimensional rounded television screen shape, still with the tube neck extending from the back. The front face of the tube fills with a live-action television image. Inside the screen, two male boxers are shown in a ring-like setting. Both are shirtless and wearing boxing gloves. One is lighter-haired or bald-looking under the lighting, and the other has darker hair. They face each other closely, raising their gloves and exchanging punches. The image is framed inside the rounded TV screen, emphasizing the clarity of the picture being shown.

More rounded television screens appear around the first one, each containing the same boxing image. They multiply across the plain background in different sizes and positions, some tilted slightly, with small tube necks extending from behind them. The repeated screens fill much of the space, suggesting that the product can be used in many television sets. The central screen remains largest, while smaller screens surround it above, below, and to the sides.

He looks like an adult man in his thirties or forties, with short dark hair, clean-shaven face, and a composed expression. He wears a dark suit jacket, white dress shirt, patterned tie, and a pocket square. He holds a small RCA-branded product carton at chest height with both hands. The box has the circular RCA logo prominently on its front. He turns it slightly and then presents it more squarely toward the viewer, as if displaying the packaging while speaking.

The presenter fades behind a dark sparkling background, leaving a large white RCA circular logo centered on screen. The logo consists of the letters “RCA” inside a circle, with a small lightning-bolt-like underline beneath the letters. The logo remains against the starry, glittering black field as the commercial closes.

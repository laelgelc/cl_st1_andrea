In black and white, a middle-aged man with light skin, a neat mustache, and closely combed, receding hair sits in a cushioned armchair in a tidy study or living room. He wears a dark suit, white shirt, and tie. Around him are traditional furnishings: a table lamp with a dark shade on his left, a fireplace and mantel behind him, bookshelves filled with volumes across the back wall, a framed picture, a small horse figurine on the mantel, and another lamp and table to the right. A small bottle and other small items sit on the side table beside him. At first he looks down, reading a magazine in his lap.

He raises his head toward the viewer, still seated upright, and brings the magazine up into view. The cover has the large title “TIME” across the top and a portrait picture below it. He holds the magazine beside his chest and taps or presses his fingers against the cover as if calling attention to a particular article. After displaying it for a moment, he lowers the magazine out of sight and leans forward slightly, speaking directly and earnestly.

As he continues, his expression becomes intent. He points with one finger toward the viewer, then glances down toward the table at his side. He reaches for a small bottle and lifts it up. The bottle then fills the screen in a close view, held between a thumb and fingers. It is a small, square glass bottle with a light-colored screw cap and a rectangular label. The label reads “Flavettes” in large letters. Beneath that, in a dark banner, it says “helps curb SMOKING and APPETITE.” Smaller print below is partly visible and appears to identify it as a tablet product from Norwich.

The view returns to the man in the chair. He looks down at the bottle in his hands, handling it carefully as though opening it or taking something from it. He then raises his hand to his mouth and places a small tablet inside. His lips close and his cheeks move slightly as he lets it dissolve. He pauses with a satisfied, knowing expression, eyebrows slightly raised, then lowers his hand.

He resumes speaking to the viewer, holding the Flavettes bottle up near his shoulder so the label faces forward. The bottle is small enough to fit easily between his fingers. He alternates between presenting it and gesturing with his free hand. His posture remains composed and professional, and his eyes return often to the viewer. At moments he holds the bottle closer to his face, as if making sure the label is clearly seen, and at other moments he lowers it briefly while continuing to explain.

Later, he brings the bottle back into a prominent position, using both hands briefly to frame or steady it. His gestures become more emphatic: he pinches his fingers together as if indicating a small tablet, then opens his hand toward the viewer. He holds the bottle beside his tie, label forward, and nods slightly while speaking. The study background stays the same, with the dark rows of books behind him and the lamp and fireplace at the sides.

Near the end, the product becomes the main visual again. A larger image of the Flavettes label rises in front of the man, filling much of the center of the screen. The words “Flavettes” and “helps curb SMOKING and APPETITE” are clearly visible. This transitions to a product-only display: the small bottle stands centered against a plain dark background, its light cap and white label sharply contrasting with the darker surroundings.

A decorative emblem reading “Norwich Products” appears superimposed over the bottle, with additional words below that appear to say “tested and tested,” though the overlap makes some of the lettering difficult to read. The emblem sits across the middle of the bottle like a seal or guarantee mark. The image then returns to the man in the study, with the Norwich emblem fading away in front of him.

For the final moments, he holds the Flavettes bottle in one hand and a cigarette in the other. The cigarette is slender and light-colored, held upright between his fingers, while the bottle is held closer to his chest with the label facing forward. He gestures between the cigarette and the bottle, then points directly to the Flavettes label, visually contrasting the two objects. He finishes looking straight ahead, calm and confident, with the product still clearly displayed in his hand.

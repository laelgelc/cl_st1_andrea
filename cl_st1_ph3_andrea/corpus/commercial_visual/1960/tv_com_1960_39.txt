The commercial opens in a very dark, reddish view of a living room, where the shapes of furniture, lamps, and seated figures are barely visible. It quickly brightens to a warmly lit domestic setting. A middle-aged man with short light hair, wearing a dark short-sleeved shirt and a patterned neck scarf, sits closely beside a young boy with a brown bowl haircut and large eyeglasses. The boy wears a light shirt under a pale sweater vest. They are on a sofa or cushioned chair in a comfortable living room with a decorated tree on the left, lamps, curtains, framed wall décor, and upholstered furniture in the background.

The man holds a large Polaroid instant camera in front of them, angled so the boy can see it. The boy watches attentively, looking from the man’s face to the camera. The man turns toward the viewer while keeping the camera in both hands, presenting it squarely forward. The camera has a boxy body with a prominent lens and viewfinder area, and a separate upright flash unit mounted on the top-right side. A strap hangs from it, and the man’s fingers steady the body as he demonstrates.

A close view fills the screen with the camera and flash attachment. The flash unit is clearly labeled “POLAROID FOCUSED FLASH.” It has a rectangular front with horizontal slatted openings. As the man manipulates the camera, the horizontal louvers inside the flash unit visibly shift through several positions, opening and closing like small blinds. The sequence emphasizes the mechanical connection between focusing the camera and changing the flash opening. The Polaroid camera body sits to the left, the flash unit to the right, with the man’s hand supporting the underside.

Back in the living room, the man holds the camera up again and faces forward, continuing the demonstration while the boy looks on. The man then lowers the camera and looks down toward his lap or the side of the chair. The camera hangs from his neck by its strap as he reaches down with one hand. He brings up a small rectangular instant photo packet or print and begins working it with both hands. His posture becomes focused and practical as he peels or separates the instant print materials.

He finishes separating the pieces and holds a white-bordered photograph in one hand while keeping another smaller piece in the other. He turns toward the boy and offers the photo. The boy takes it and raises it near his face, examining it.

A close view of the finished Polaroid photograph appears. A hand holds the white-bordered print by the upper-right edge. Inside the photo, the boy is pictured sitting on a patterned chair with his knees drawn up, looking straight ahead. The image is dark-toned but evenly visible, showing his face, sweater vest, and plaid trousers clearly against the busy chair pattern.

The commercial returns to the boy’s face in the living room. He holds the print just below his chin and looks sideways, appearing thoughtful and a little curious. Then the man and boy are seen together again. The boy keeps the photograph in his hand while the man, with the camera still hanging at his chest, holds the separated backing or discard piece. They face each other and converse casually.

At the end, large white serif text appears over the living room scene, centered across the man, boy, and camera. It reads: “Polaroid’s Focused Flash 400 Land Cameras.” The man and boy remain seated side by side behind the text, with the Polaroid camera still visible hanging from the man’s neck.

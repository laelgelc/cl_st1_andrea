A dark, futuristic room appears, with smooth curved walls and a sculptural white lounge chair on the left. A dark silhouetted figure reclines in the chair, one hand lifted. In the center, on a round white pedestal, a bright column of light glows around the Touché game board, so bright that it washes the room in white. As the glow fades, a gray-haired, older adult man appears standing on the pedestal. He wears a shiny silver, space-suit-like outfit with dark stripes down the sleeves and legs. He turns toward the seated silhouette, as if summoned to play.

The scene shifts to the game itself. The Touché board sits on a white plastic base with a dark blue square grid in the middle. Along the edges are small clear game pieces shaped like short transparent cylinders on round bases. Some pieces contain colored disks, red on one side and white on the other. A hand reaches in near the lower edge of the board, where a red rectangular area and small red buttons are visible, then gestures above the grid as if explaining the rules.

The silver-suited man is shown from the chest up, facing forward with wide eyes and an open mouth, looking surprised or intrigued. Then he leans over the round table, watching the board closely. He gestures with one hand, concentrating on the game.

A close view of the board shows a hand lifting one of the clear pieces from the side and moving it onto a square of the blue grid. The transparent piece is set down carefully, revealing a red disk inside at the bottom. The piece sits upright on the grid, its round base resting on one square.

Back at the table, the silver-suited man watches tensely. A dark, gloved or shadowed hand, belonging to the unseen opponent at the left, reaches toward the board and places or moves another clear piece. In close-up, the piece that was red is shown inside its transparent casing; suddenly the disk changes from red to white, demonstrating the game’s hidden magnetic “force” that can flip a piece to the opponent’s color. The silver-suited man reacts with surprise, bringing his hand to his mouth.

The board fills the view again. The word “touché” appears in large white lowercase letters across the dark blue grid. Around it are the clear game pieces with red and white disks. A hand moves one piece from an upper square to another square on the grid. As it is pressed into place, the color inside changes to white. The board then appears with several pieces scattered across the grid: some show red disks, some show white disks, and some sit on the edge areas.

The silver-suited man becomes animated and pleased. He throws his hands up and then claps or rubs his hands together over the table, smiling broadly. He looks down at the board, then toward his opponent, clearly delighted with the outcome. His posture is upright and excited, and his shiny suit catches the soft white light of the minimalist room.

Near the end, the dark silhouette of the opponent rises or leans into the foreground at the edge of the table while the man continues smiling with his hands clasped. A final close view shows a clear game piece on the blue board: the disk inside is white at first, then flips to red. Over the close-up, a white rectangular “Gabriel” logo appears with black lettering and a horizontal rainbow stripe beneath it, identifying the maker.

Outside at night, a brightly lit storefront fills the background. Large red lettering on the front window reads “Chicken Lickin’,” with smaller red words underneath that appear to say “You Ring We Bring.” The street number “874” is visible above a glass door. Shelves and containers can be seen through the window, while the dark silhouette of a car passes across the foreground, partly blocking the lower right of the shopfront.

Inside the restaurant kitchen, a young adult man in a white short-sleeved work uniform and apron stands by stainless-steel counters and equipment. He wears a tall white paper hat decorated like a chicken comb or feathers, with a yellow band and red tip. He holds a black corded telephone receiver to his ear with one hand and grips the handle of a mop with the other. Behind him are metal shelves, pots, stacked plates, and restaurant supplies. His expression is focused and slightly weary as he looks down and talks into the phone, then glances aside as if responding to someone on the line.

A wider view shows the kitchen floor around him: a large blue bucket sits in front, and nearby is a plastic bottle labeled “Liquid Cleaner.” The man still has the telephone at his ear while standing with the mop, suggesting he has been cleaning with the liquid cleaner. Later, he stands in front of a dark window with red lettering from the restaurant sign visible backward from inside. He holds the mop handle across his body, listening on the phone with a serious, uncertain look.

The commercial then visually shifts to the cleaning product. An orange box of Spic and Span is held over a yellow bucket filled with yellowish water. The box is tipped, and white powder pours first into a small cap or scoop held over the bucket, then into the water. The powder falls in a thick stream and hits the surface, creating cloudy white swirls that billow through the water. The white clouds spread and mix, suggesting the cleaner dissolving and “powering” the water.

Back in the restaurant kitchen, the man mops a large, pale clean-looking area on a heavily soiled, mottled floor. The surrounding floor is streaked with brownish grime, while the area under the mop is much lighter. He pushes a yellow sponge mop with a long handle across the floor. The yellow bucket sits to one side, and the blue bucket remains nearby. The man bends over the mop, working the sponge head back and forth over the dirty surface.

He lifts the mop up in front of himself and examines it. The mop head is shown clearly: a yellow rectangular sponge sits on a black metal or plastic wringer plate with rows of small white triangular openings. Droplets cling to the sponge. The man looks at the mop head while still holding the telephone, as though checking what the cleaner has lifted from the floor. His expression shifts from concern to a more satisfied, knowing look.

The final image places the orange Spic and Span box prominently on a counter beside a black telephone receiver lying off the hook. The package reads “Spic Span” in large blue letters and “THE BIG JOB CLEANER” near the bottom. Behind it are stacked plates and shelves in the restaurant kitchen. To the right of the box, large white on-screen text reads: “Gets the dirt liquid cleaners leave behind.”

The commercial opens on the exterior of a house at night in winter. Snow covers the ground, the front steps, and the edges of a small porch with white railings and square posts. Dark evergreen shrubs frame the entrance. Warm amber light glows from the windows and from a small porch light, contrasting with the cold blue light on the snow. Icicles hang along the roofline, reinforcing the freezing outdoor setting. A glass-paneled front door on the left stands partly open or reveals warm light from inside, but no person or animal is visible outside.

The snowy porch remains the focus for several moments. The house looks inviting from within, with curtains and windowpanes lit in orange tones, while the steps and walkway stay empty and cold. The visual emphasis is on the contrast between the harsh winter exterior and the warm interior suggested behind the door and windows.

The image then blends into a close view of a home heating unit. A tall, light-colored Carrier furnace fills the center, set among metal ductwork and piping. On the front panel, a dark label bears the blue oval Carrier logo and the name “WeatherMaker” in orange lettering; smaller text beneath identifies it as a high-efficiency gas furnace. The unit appears clean and upright, presented like the source of the indoor comfort being referred to in the commercial.

The furnace image fades back to the same snowy front porch. The amber windows continue to glow warmly, while the porch steps remain covered with snow. The open, lit doorway and the warm interior light visually suggest that staying inside is more comfortable than going out into the cold. A translucent Carrier logo and the slogan “We’re The Inside Guys.” begin to appear over the house exterior.

The commercial then shifts fully to a blue graphic screen. Centered is a round, old-fashioned thermostat dial, with the Carrier blue oval logo placed across it. Beneath it, in white serif lettering, the slogan reads: “We’re The Inside Guys.” The thermostat remains centered against a deep blue gradient background, emphasizing indoor temperature control.

The thermostat imagery transforms into a circular gas-burner-like graphic seen from above. Dark crossbars form a stovetop grate shape over a pale circular center. Blue flames then appear evenly around the ring, radiating outward in a symmetrical pattern. The image becomes increasingly stylized, with bright blue flame rays spreading from the circular burner against the blue background.

Finally, the burner graphic fades into a simple blue natural gas flame icon centered on the screen. White text beneath it reads: “Clean natural gas. America’s best energy value.” A smaller line below says: “SPONSORED BY AMERICAN GAS ASSOCIATION.” The final image is clean and minimal, with the flame symbol and text standing out against the dark blue field.

The commercial opens inside a bright Sears appliance showroom. Five Sears employees stand together in a confident, posed lineup beneath a large blue-and-white “Sears” sign. They wear work attire in Sears blue: mostly royal blue polo shirts with name badges or lanyards, along with black pants. Two men at the left wear lighter blue button-down shirts. The group includes men and women of different appearances; one woman with dark hair stands near the center with her hands on her hips, and another woman with red hair stands at the far right in the same hands-on-hips pose. Behind them are rows of appliances and overhead fluorescent lights, giving the store a clean, polished retail look.

A blue-white glow washes over the group, turning them into darker silhouettes as the scene shifts into a graphic title card. Their silhouettes remain grouped on the left, casting long shadows across a white background. Large bold blue text appears beside them: “Sears BLUE APPLIANCE CREW,” with “BLUE” especially large and prominent.

The action moves back into the appliance department. A casually dressed male customer, about young to middle-aged, with short dark hair, a gray zip hoodie, olive T-shirt, jeans, and sneakers, walks toward a Sears employee in a blue polo shirt. They meet near a tall blue-and-white kiosk labeled “Sears Research Center.” A computer monitor sits on the kiosk counter. Around them are front-loading washers and dryers in glossy colors, including teal, red, dark blue, silver, and white. A hanging sign also reads “Sears Research Center,” reinforcing that this is a service area inside the store.

The customer is shown closer, speaking with a thoughtful, slightly concerned expression. Behind him, the store remains bright and orderly, with appliance displays and blue Sears department signs visible in the background. He faces the employee as if explaining what he needs.

A product display highlights a matching pair of dark blue LG front-loading laundry machines. They sit side by side, each with a round chrome-trimmed door and the “LG” logo clearly visible on the glass. Their glossy finish reflects the store lights, presenting them as the appliance pair under discussion.

Back at the Sears Research Center, the employee in the blue Sears polo talks with the customer. The employee stands open and attentive, wearing a lanyard and badge, while the customer faces him with his hands near his sides. The employee then steps to the kiosk computer and begins using the mouse or keyboard. The customer stands beside him watching. The monitor is angled toward them, and the blue “Sears Research Center” sign remains directly above and beside the workstation. In the background, another blue-shirted Sears employee assists near red laundry machines.

As the employee works at the computer, he turns his head and gestures off to the side, drawing the customer’s attention away from the kiosk. The customer follows his look.

The scene cuts to a comic-looking area inside the Sears store, set up near the front doors under another large “Sears” sign. A ping-pong table stands in the middle, with several casually dressed men around it. One man in a striped polo shirt stands behind the table, while another man in a light striped shirt is positioned at the right as if playing. The original customer in the gray hoodie stands at the left edge, watching. Blue rope barriers on metal stanchions separate this area from the rest of the showroom, and appliances are displayed along the sides. A couch or seating area sits to the left, adding to the feeling that these men have been waiting or killing time inside the store.

The view returns to the customer and the Sears employee at the Research Center computer. The employee has turned back toward the customer, while the monitor behind them shows a webpage-like layout with product images and text, suggesting comparison or research information. The customer looks toward the employee, appearing reassured or impressed.

A closer view isolates one of the men from the ping-pong area: a middle-aged man with short thinning hair and glasses, wearing a blue-green striped polo shirt. He stands in front of Sears-branded glass doors, looking downward with a tired or resigned posture, visually contrasting with the quick help the main customer is receiving.

A clean white graphic card appears. At the top, large gray-blue text reads, “WE DO THE WORK.” Near the lower right, the silhouetted Sears Blue Appliance Crew stands beside the bold “BLUE CREW” graphic. Small fine print near the bottom left says to see the store for guarantee terms and conditions.

The promotional offer follows on another white card. Large text reads, “20% OFF TOP BRANDS” and beneath it, “10% OFF ALL OTHER BRANDS.” Brand logos appear above a lineup of appliances, including a stainless refrigerator, a stove or oven range, and a front-loading washer and dryer. Fine print runs along the bottom, with a larger line reading, “FOR FULL OFFER SEE SEARS.COM.”

The commercial ends on a mostly white screen with the blue “Sears” logo centered and “SEARS.COM” printed near the lower right.

The commercial opens on a bright orange screen with the Vonage logo centered in white and blue, with the small words “sounds good” underneath. The screen then shifts to a plain yellow field with no people or objects visible.

A large burger appears against a vivid red background, sitting on a sheet of tan parchment. The burger has a glossy golden-brown bun, melted yellow cheese, a thick beef patty, red onion rings, a tomato slice, green lettuce, pickle slices, and yellow mustard visible near the bottom bun. A large knife is stuck vertically through the top bun, its black handle pointing upward. Bold white text appears to the left of the burger reading “MADE IT BIGGER,” with “BIGGER” especially large and blocky.

The image switches to a bright green background where a large white price begins to appear, first showing “$6.” Then the burger is shown very close against a yellow background, filling the right side of the screen so the layers are prominent: shiny bun, cheese draped over the patty, onion, tomato, lettuce, pickles, and mustard. The full price then appears in large white numerals on a red background: “$6.99.”

The meal is presented on a rectangular metal tray against a green background. On the left side of the tray sits the same burger with the knife through it; on the right is a metal cup or basket lined with white paper and filled with French fries. Near the fries is a small round cup holding sliced green pickles or a green garnish. White text appears above and around the meal, first “FOR,” then expanding to “FOR AN OLDTIMER WITH CHEESE,” identifying the burger shown. The words are large, bold, and white, with “CHEESE” especially prominent.

A yellow screen follows with the single word “ALSO” in large white letters. The burger returns against a red background, again on parchment with the knife through the bun. Small fine print appears near the bottom but is too small to read clearly. White promotional text builds on the left side until it reads “CHILI’S NEW BIGGER BIG MOUTH BURGERS,” while the burger remains large on the right, emphasizing its height and stacked ingredients.

At the end, the full tray meal appears again on a yellow background: burger on the left, fries in a metal cup on the right, and the small green side cup near the front. The knife still stands upright through the burger bun. The word “BABY” appears in large white letters near the upper left, matching the energetic visual style of the earlier text. No people are shown; the commercial focuses entirely on the burger, its price, and the plated meal presentation.

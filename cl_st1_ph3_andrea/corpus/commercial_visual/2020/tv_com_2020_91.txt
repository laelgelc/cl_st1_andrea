The commercial opens on a flat SIXT-orange background with small social-media-style complaints centered near the upper left. One reads, “my rental car is not fun to drive,” and another below it says, “this rental car SUCKS,” with tiny reply, repost, heart, and share icons underneath. A small SIXT watermark sits in the lower left. The background switches to dark charcoal, showing another tweet-like post in orange text: “This rental car is so,” cut off before the thought is finished.

Large bold words then take over the screen in a fast graphic sequence. On orange, black text reads “LET’S BE,” followed by a huge white “HONEST,” then the same word “HONEST” in orange on a dark background. The typography continues with single words and phrases appearing one after another: “THE,” “INDUSTRY,” “IS THE,” and then a grey sleeping-face emoji with little “Z” marks above it. The word “BORING” fills the screen in enormous block letters, first orange on dark, then black on orange, with the sleepy emoji perched over the letters. More graphic text follows: “AND THE,” “CAN BE FOUND,” an oversized cropped “BE,” and “THE NAME.”

A series of plain logo-like title cards shows different versions of the phrase “rent-a-car” or “Rent a Car” in various fonts and colors: white italic text on dark grey, black lowercase on yellow, grey title-case on white, thin white uppercase on red, and white title-case on green. The repetition emphasizes the generic phrase visually. Then a pale computer-search-style box appears on a light background with the word “Friend” typed inside and a small mouse cursor below it.

The scene changes to a bright beach. Two young women, seen from behind, run barefoot toward the ocean carrying surfboards over their heads. The woman on the left has long dark hair, a dark bikini top, and light denim shorts; the woman on the right wears a light patterned top and black shorts. Waves roll in ahead of them under a pale sky. Huge overlaid text appears across the beach image: “THE” in white on the left, then “FRIEND” in orange on the right, forming “THE FRIEND.”

Next, inside a grey office cubicle, a blond young man sits at a curved desk facing an older desktop computer. The desk holds office items including a lamp, cups, pens, papers, and a water bottle. On the computer screen, a car image is replaced by a simple list that repeats “A JOB” several times in black text, making the work setting look ordinary and repetitive.

The imagery immediately contrasts with an outdoor skydiving scene. A tandem skydiving pair floats belly-down high above the ground. The front participant, a smiling woman with hair blown back, is strapped to an instructor behind her, who wears an orange helmet, goggles, gloves, and a harness. Their arms are spread in freefall against a wide sky with clouds. Large text overlays the sky: “THE” in white on the left and “JOB” in orange on the right, forming “THE JOB.”

The screen returns to orange graphics, dominated by a huge white “THE” on the left. Smaller black words begin to build beneath it, showing “IS” and then the start of “IS ALWA…,” while a large white rectangular block sits below, as if the phrase is being constructed piece by piece. A dark card then appears with the words “A CAR” in white, spaced apart, small against the empty background.

A teal-green performance sedan appears driving directly toward the viewer along an industrial waterfront road. Its bright headlight shapes are visible, with warehouse-like buildings on the left, water and cranes to the right, and a tall crane structure ahead. The car looks low, wide, and sporty, with a black grille and sculpted hood. As it moves, dust or road grit trails behind. The view shifts to the car from the side and rear as it speeds along the tracks or roadway beside the industrial buildings. Large overlaid words appear around it: “THE” in white on the left and “CAR” in orange on the right, spelling “THE CAR.”

The teal sedan is then shown on an open paved road or track in a sunnier, more open setting, with low greenery in the background. It drives past from front three-quarter view to side view, its wheels spinning and its dark grille and sleek body lines clearly visible.

The commercial ends on a solid orange SIXT brand card. A large black “SIXT” logo with a white accent sits above the words “rent a car.” Along the bottom is a lineup of several rental vehicles, including white, blue, black, and grey cars and SUVs, arranged like a showroom display. A small black rectangle near the bottom center reads “SIXT.COM.”

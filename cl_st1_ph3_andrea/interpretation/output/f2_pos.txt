## Factor f2 (positive pole): interpretation as a discourse dimension

The positive pole of Factor 2 appears to encode a discourse of **digitally mediated domestic life**, in which everyday household, leisure, shopping, work, safety, entertainment, and service activities are visually organised around screens, apps, devices, and interface overlays. This pole is driven especially by the **2020s**, with a clear diachronic rise from the 2000s onward.

### Suggested labels

Possible labels for this pole include:

1. **“Networked Domestic Life”**  
2. **“App-Mediated Home and Everyday Consumption”**  
3. **“Screen-Saturated Domesticity”**  
4. **“Connected Household Interfaces”**  
5. **“Platformised Everyday Living”**

The most concise label would be:

> **Networked Domestic Life**

A slightly more explicit label would be:

> **App-Mediated Domestic Consumption and Services**

---

## Justification from decade scores

The decade scores show a strong chronological pattern:

| Decade | Mean f2 score |
|---|---:|
| 1950s | 1.46 |
| 1960s | 1.47 |
| 1970s | 1.34 |
| 1980s | 1.97 |
| 1990s | 2.35 |
| 2000s | 3.56 |
| 2010s | 5.39 |
| 2020s | 6.70 |

The pole is most strongly associated with the **2020s**, followed by the **2010s** and then the **2000s**. This suggests that the discourse becomes increasingly prominent as commercials move into the era of smartphones, app ecosystems, online shopping, streaming media, smart-home services, digital work, and platform-based customer interaction.

The lower but still positive scores in earlier decades indicate that some lexical items at this pole, such as *home*, *room*, *tv*, *couch*, or *living*, can occur in earlier advertising too. However, the much higher scores in the 2010s and especially the 2020s show that this pole is primarily driven by a more recent visual style: the home as a digitally connected environment.

This cluster points very strongly to visual descriptions involving digital screens, app pages, computer interfaces, icons, widgets, dashboards, and graphic overlays. Words such as *floating*, *icon*, *header*, *page*, *desktop*, and *screen* suggest the now-common commercial style where digital content is shown as a visible layer in the scene: floating app panels, enlarged phone screens, simulated webpages, translucent dashboards, or interface elements superimposed over physical space.

The pole therefore includes not only domestic technology but also the broader **platformisation of everyday consumer activity**: ordering, searching, shopping, streaming, contacting, tracking, and managing household tasks through digital services.

## Justification from the examples

The high-scoring examples are all from the **2020s**, which confirms that the 2020s drive this pole especially strongly. The examples repeatedly combine domestic space, digital devices, app interfaces, and everyday service use.

### Example 1: domestic work/life mediated by devices

In f2_pos_001, the commercial is explicitly described as “urban, domestic, and mobile work/life scenes connected by the use of digital devices.” The loading words include:

- *interface, couch, laptop, smartphone, device, app, digital, safety, phone, home, icon, remote, room, connect, domestic, shelf, wooden, screen, multiple, sit*

The visuals move through home dining areas, kitchens, children using tablets, adults using laptops, and a woman sitting on a couch with a Microsoft laptop. This is a prototypical instance of the pole: domestic life organised around connected screens.

### Example 2: floating interfaces and app ecosystems

f2_pos_002 is a Windows 11-style commercial featuring:

- a laptop in a living room,
- floating app-like panels,
- icons resembling Facebook, Adobe Creative Cloud, and Xbox,
- translucent dashboards,
- weather widgets,
- messaging panels,
- simulated digital spaces.

Its loading words include:

- *interface, living, laptop, live, digital, sofa, floating, icon, header, multicolored, room, shelving, information, space, domestic, shelf, screen*

This example strongly illustrates the *interface/floating/icon/screen* side of the loadings. It expands the domestic digital setting into a visually spectacular hybrid of home, virtual environment, game world, and productivity interface.

### Example 3: app-based home improvement

f2_pos_003 centres on a woman using a smartphone and a Home Depot app to plan household renovation or ordering. Relevant loading words include:

- *interface, smartphone, page, app, phone, home, icon, header, room, key, delivery, screen*

The commercial visually presents home improvement as an app-mediated activity: calculating materials, viewing product pages, ordering supplies, and connecting the physical home project with digital shopping tools.

### Example 4: living room, phone, TV, store, and pet safety

f2_pos_004 begins in a living room with a sofa, plants, shelves, dog, smartphone, TV screen, remote, and coffee table. It then moves to a store display and product-use scenes. Loading words include:

This example shows how even a lawn/garden product is presented through a contemporary domestic-media environment: the pet watches a TV ad, interacts with a phone-like screen, and the product is framed through safety, pet-friendliness, and retail display.

### Example 6: app-enabled neighbourhood safety and connection

f2_pos_006, the Ring “Search Party” commercial, is especially important for the *connect/safety/information/home/dog/app* part of the pole. It includes:

- smartphone screens,

This shows the pole’s discourse of digital connection extending from the home into the neighbourhood: the household is linked to a platformed community network.

### Example 8: streaming and living-room media control

f2_pos_008 is a DIRECTV Stream commercial. It begins with a couple sitting on a sofa in a bright living room, using a remote and watching a TV screen. Loading words include:

This example represents the entertainment side of the pole: domestic leisure, streaming services, remote controls, television screens, and branded end cards.

### Example 9: Amazon home shopping and delivery

f2_pos_009 presents Amazon shopping as part of everyday domestic and neighbourhood life. It includes people on couches or beds with smartphones, a home office with laptop and phone, an Amazon shopping page, and a final doorstep delivery. Loading words include:

This is a clear example of **app-mediated consumption**: household needs, digital browsing, online purchasing, and delivery are integrated into ordinary home scenes.

### Example 10: TurboTax app and mobile work/life flow

f2_pos_010 combines apartment life, a laptop, email interfaces, a smartphone notification, and TurboTax app screens. Loading words include:

## Discourse encoded at this pole

This pole represents a visual-commercial discourse in which the home is no longer simply a private domestic setting. It is depicted as a **connected command centre** for work, shopping, entertainment, security, communication, household management, and service access.

The recurring visual pattern is:

- people sitting in domestic spaces;
- phones, laptops, tablets, televisions, and remotes close at hand;
- app screens and digital pages shown in close-up;
- floating or overlaid interface graphics;
- online services connected to physical outcomes such as delivery, shopping, home improvement, safety alerts, or entertainment access;
- pets, children, couches, plants, shelves, and coffee tables grounding the technology in everyday home life.

The pole therefore encodes a discourse of **ordinary life as digitally assisted, platform-connected, and screen-mediated**.

---

## Diachronic interpretation

The rising decade scores suggest that this discourse becomes increasingly central in television-commercial visuals from the **2000s onward**, accelerates in the **2010s**, and is most dominant in the **2020s**.

This trajectory corresponds to broader changes in advertising visuals:

- the spread of home computers and laptops;
- the normalization of smartphones;
- the rise of apps and platform services;
- streaming media and smart entertainment systems;
- online retail and delivery;
- smart-home and home-security services;
- hybrid work and home-based digital productivity;
- interface graphics becoming a common visual storytelling device.

The 2020s examples particularly intensify this discourse by presenting digital interfaces not merely as objects on screens but as visual environments: floating panels, translucent dashboards, app icons, simulated phone pages, and network graphics become part of the commercial’s visual world.

---

## Preferred label

The best overall label for f2 positive is:

> **Networked Domestic Life**

This label captures both halves of the factor: the **domestic setting** signalled by words such as *couch, living, sofa, home, room, domestic, shelf, plant, coffee,* and *dog*, and the **networked digital interface layer** signalled by *interface, laptop, smartphone, app, digital, page, icon, computer, device, phone,* and *screen*.

If a more consumption-oriented label is desired, a strong alternative would be:

> **App-Mediated Domestic Consumption and Services**

This second label foregrounds the role of online shopping, delivery, streaming, home improvement, pet safety, tax services, and digital platforms in the high-scoring examples.

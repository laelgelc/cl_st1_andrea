## Interpretation of Factor f3 (positive pole)

Factor f3 positive appears to encode a discourse of **highly stylised, technologically mediated visual spectacle**, especially associated with **1990s advertising aesthetics**: videogame imagery, futuristic environments, rapid montage, saturated or high-contrast lighting, reflective/glossy surfaces, silhouettes, flashes, overexposure, and graphic layering.

A suitable main label would be:

### **Techno-futurist visual spectacle and stylised montage**

Other possible labels include:

1. **Digital/gaming spectacle and high-contrast visual stylisation**  
2. **Futuristic graphic environments and luminous montage**  
3. **1990s techno-visual intensity and cinematic advertising style**  
4. **Stylised light, speed, and digital-interface imagery**  
5. **Glossy futuristic spectacle with layered graphics and game footage**

## Justification from decade scores

The decade scores show that this pole is most strongly driven by the **1990s**:

| Decade | Mean f3 |
|---|---:|
| 1990s | **5.23** |
| 2000s | 3.28 |
| 1980s | 2.88 |
| 1960s | 2.81 |
| 2020s | 2.80 |
| 1970s | 2.42 |
| 2010s | 2.33 |
| 1950s | 2.00 |

All decades have positive mean scores, so the features associated with this pole occur throughout the visual-commercial subcorpus to some extent. However, the **1990s stand out very clearly**, with a mean far above all other decades. The high-scoring examples are also all from the 1990s, which strongly suggests that this pole captures a visual discourse especially characteristic of that decade.

Diachronically, the pattern is not a simple linear increase. Instead, it suggests a **1990s peak** in a particular kind of commercial visuality: fast, glossy, digitally inflected, youth-oriented, technologically spectacular advertising. The 2000s remain relatively high, implying some continuation of this style, while the 2010s dip and the 2020s rise only moderately. This points to a historically concentrated visual regime rather than a steady long-term trend.

## Overall discourse encoded by the pole

This positive pole represents commercials whose visual descriptions foreground **spectacle as visual design**. The discourse is one of:

- technological futurity;
- videogame and screen culture;
- rapid montage and switching;
- graphic layering and title-card aesthetics;
- intense lighting, flashes, silhouettes, and overexposure;
- glossy, reflective, metallic, or polished surfaces;
- stylised architectural/studio environments;
- dynamic action, speed, and sensory impact;
- visually constructed brand worlds rather than everyday realism.

The pole is especially characteristic of the **1990s**, where the examples show a convergence of videogame advertising, MTV-like editing, digital-interface aesthetics, neon/gloss colour palettes, and cinematic brand stylisation. It appears to capture a moment when television commercials increasingly presented products not simply as objects, but as entries into **immersive, high-energy, technologically enhanced visual environments**.

## Recommended label

The strongest label for f3 positive is:

### **Techno-futurist visual spectacle and stylised montage**

This label captures both major sides of the pole: the **technological/gaming/futuristic vocabulary** in the loadings and examples, and the broader **stylised lighting, montage, reflection, silhouette, and graphic-environment aesthetic** that appears across videogame, automobile, fashion, beauty, and lifestyle commercials.

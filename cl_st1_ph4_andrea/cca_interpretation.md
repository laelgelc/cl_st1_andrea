Because the loadings for `ver1`, `ver3`, `ver2`, `vis3`, and `vis7` are all **positive**, they define the same pole of the canonical dimension. In other words, commercials that score high on this canonical dimension tend to combine these verbal and visual patterns together.

### Substantive interpretation

This canonical dimension appears to capture a **strong alignment between verbal advertising discourse and visual advertising style**. The verbal side is mainly driven by `ver1`, with additional support from `ver3` and `ver2`, which means that the dimension is especially anchored in the first verbal discourse dimension but broadened by two other verbal patterns.

The visual side is even more sharply structured: `vis3` and `vis7` are both very strong positive contributors, while `vis1` is a weaker but meaningful negative contributor. This suggests a cross-modal opposition between:

- a **positive pole**, where the verbal dimensions `ver1`, `ver3`, and `ver2` align with the visual dimensions `vis3` and `vis7`;
- and an **opposing pole**, associated with the inverse of `vis1`.

So this canonical dimension is not simply “verbal dimension 1 plus visual dimension 3.” Rather, it represents a broader **multimodal pattern**: a constellation of verbal discourses that co-occurs with a constellation of visual features.
